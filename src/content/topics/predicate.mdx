## Overview

The predicate expresses what is said about the subject.

- **Spanish**: Verbal predicates are bare. Adjectival and nominal predicates require *ser* or *estar*. Distinguishes inherent (*ser*) vs state (*estar*).
- **English**: Verbal predicates are bare. All non-verbal predicates require *be*. Uses *become, seem, get* as pseudo-copular verbs.
- **Chinese**: Adjectival predicates are bare (no copula). Nominal predicates require *<ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>*. Progressive uses *<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>*. Resultative change uses *<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>*.

<div class="spanish-section">

## Spanish

### Verbal predicates

- *Juan **lee**.* (intransitive)
- *Juan **lee** un libro.* (transitive)

### Adjectival predicates

**Ser** (inherent, permanent):

- *Juan **es** alto.* (John is tall.)
- *María **es** inteligente.*

**Estar** (state, location, progressive):

- *Juan **está** cansado.* (tired — state)
- *El libro **está** en la mesa.* (location)
- *El perro **está** ladrando.* (progressive)

**Ser vs estar**:

| | Ser | Estar |
|---|---|---|
| aburrido | Él es aburrido. (boring) | Está aburrido. (bored) |
| verde | Es verde. (green) | Está verde. (unripe) |
| vivo | Es vivo. (lively) | Está vivo. (alive) |
| listo | Es listo. (clever) | Está listo. (ready) |

### Nominal predicates

Always *ser*:

- *Juan **es** profesor.*
- *Esto **es** un problema.*

### Progressive

- *Juan **está leyendo**.*
- *Estábamos **comiendo**.*

### Resultative

- *Se **puso** furioso.* (got furious)
- *Se **volvió** loco.* (went crazy)
- *Llegó a ser famoso.* (became famous)

### Pseudo-copular

- *Parece **feliz**.* (seems happy)
- *Sigue **siendo** importante.* (remains important)

<div class="english-section">

## English

### Verbal predicates

- *John **reads**.* (intransitive)
- *John **reads** a book.* (transitive)

### Adjectival predicates

Always with *be*:

- *John **is** tall.*
- *The soup **is** hot.*

**Get / become / turn / go + adjective**:

- *He **got** angry.*
- *She **became** famous.*
- *The milk **turned** sour.*
- *He **went** crazy.*
- *The leaves **turned** red.*

### Nominal predicates

Always with *be*:

- *John **is** a teacher.*
- *This **is** a problem.*

### Progressive

- *John **is reading**.*
- *They **were eating**.*

### Passive

- *The book **was written** by John.*

### Resultative

- *The situation **became** serious.*
- *He **grew** old.*
- *She **fell** ill.*

### Pseudo-copular verbs

| Verb | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| seem | He seems happy. | apparent state |
| appear | He appears tired. | apparent state |
| become | He became angry. | change of state |
| get | He got angry. | change of state |
| turn | The milk turned sour. | change of state |
| go | He went crazy. | negative change |
| grow | He grew old. | gradual change |
| remain | He remained silent. | continuation |
| stay | She stayed calm. | continuation |
| look | You look great. | appearance |
| sound | That sounds good. | auditory impression |
| feel | I feel sick. | tactile/emotional impression |

### Object complements

- *He made her **happy**.* (adjectival)
- *They elected him **president**.* (nominal)
- *I found the book **interesting**.* (adjectival)

## Chinese

### Verbal predicates

Bare verbs:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(He reads.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby>。(He eats.)

### Adjectival predicates (no copula)

Chinese adjectives function as predicates without a copula:

- <ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby>。(John is tall.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>。(This is good.)
- <ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>气<rt>qì</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>冷<rt>lěng</rt></ruby>。(The weather is cold.)

**Note**: A degree adverb (<ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby>, <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>非<rt>fēi</rt></ruby><ruby>常<rt>cháng</rt></ruby>) is typically required. Without it, the adjective has a comparative/exclamatory sense:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby>！(He's tall! — exclamatory/comparative)

### Nominal predicates (copula required)

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby>。(He is a teacher.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(This is a book.)

### Progressive

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(He is reading.)
- <ruby>狗<rt>gǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>叫<rt>jiào</rt></ruby>。(The dog is barking.)

### Locative predicate

- <ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>。(The book is on the table.)

### Resultative change

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby><ruby>气<rt>qì</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He got angry. — <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> marks change)
- <ruby>她<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>累<rt>lèi</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(She got tired.)

### Causative resultative

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>让<rt>ràng</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>兴<rt>xìng</rt></ruby>。(He made me happy.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>消<rt>xiāo</rt></ruby><ruby>息<rt>xi</rt></ruby><ruby>让<rt>ràng</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>难<rt>nán</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby>。(This news made him sad.)

## Comparison at a glance

| Predicate type | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verbal | Bare verb | Bare verb | Bare verb |
| Adjectival | ser/estar + adjective | be + adjective | Adjective directly ( + degree adv) |
| Nominal | ser + noun | be + noun | <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> + noun |
| Progressive | estar + -ndo | be + -ing | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> + verb |
| Locative | estar + PP | be + PP | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> + location |
| Resultative | ponerse/volverse | become/get/turn | Verb + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> |
| Passive | ser + participle | be + participle | <ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby> + verb |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby>**<ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby>**<ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby>* (no <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> with adjectives)

2. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *He teacher* → *He **is** a teacher* (copula required)

3. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *El perro es ladrando* → *El perro **está** ladrando* (progressive uses estar)

4. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *He became to be angry* → *He **became** angry* (no to-infinitive)
