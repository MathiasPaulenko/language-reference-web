## Overview

Result clauses express consequences: so tired that I fell asleep, too small to reach.

- **Spanish**: Uses *tan + adjective/adverb + que* and *tanto + noun + que*. The subjunctive may appear for hypothetical results. *Demasiado...para* expresses "too...to."
- **English**: Uses *so + adjective/adverb + that* and *such + (a/an) + adjective + noun + that*. "Too...to" and "...enough to" are also common.
- **Chinese**: Uses *<ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby> + adjective + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* for "too." Result is introduced with *<ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>至<rt>zhì</rt></ruby><ruby>于<rt>yú</rt></ruby>* or simply juxtaposed. *<ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>/<ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>* equates to "so."

## Spanish

### Tan...que (so...that)

With adjectives and adverbs:

- *Es **tan alto que** toca el techo.* (He's so tall that he touches the ceiling.)
- *Corre **tan rápido que** nadie puede alcanzarlo.* (He runs so fast that no one can catch him.)
- *Está **tan cansado que** se durmió.* (He's so tired that he fell asleep.)

### Tanto...que (so much...that)

With nouns and verbs:

- *Tiene **tanto dinero que** no sabe qué hacer con él.* (He has so much money that he doesn't know what to do with it.)
- *Comió **tanto que** le dolía el estómago.* (He ate so much that his stomach hurt.)
- *Llovió **tanto que** se inundó la calle.* (It rained so much that the street flooded.)

**Agreement**: *tanto* agrees with the noun:

- *tanta agua* (fem)
- *tantos libros* (masc pl)
- *tantas personas* (fem pl)

### Demasiado...para (too...to)

- *Es **demasiado alto para** entrar.* (He's too tall to enter.)
- *Es **demasiado caro para** comprarlo.* (It's too expensive to buy.)
- *Llegó **demasiado tarde para** verla.* (He arrived too late to see her.)

### Lo suficientemente...para / bastante...para (enough to)

- *Es **lo suficientemente alto para** tocarlo.* (He's tall enough to touch it.)
- *Es **bastante inteligente para** entenderlo.* (He's smart enough to understand it.)
- *No es **lo suficientemente bueno para** ganar.* (He's not good enough to win.)

### Subjunctive in result clauses

When the result is hypothetical, potential, or future:

- *Es tan difícil **que no pueda** terminar.* (It's so difficult that I might not finish. — subjunctive: possibility)
- *Es tan difícil **que no puedo** terminar.* (It's so difficult that I can't finish. — indicative: certainty)

## English

### So...that

With adjectives and adverbs:

- *He is **so tall that** he hits his head on the doorframe.*
- *She spoke **so quietly that** I couldn't hear her.*
- *It was **so cold that** the lake froze.*

**So + much/many/little/few + noun**:

- *He has **so much money that** he doesn't know what to do with it.*
- *There were **so many people that** we couldn't move.*
- *I have **so little time that** I can't finish.*

### Such...that

With noun phrases:

- *It was **such a good movie that** I watched it twice.*
- *He is **such a good person that** everyone loves him.*
- *They faced **such difficulties that** they almost gave up.*

**Such + adjective + plural/uncountable noun**:

- *They are **such good students that** the teacher is proud.*
- *It was **such beautiful weather that** we went to the park.*

### Too...to

- *He is **too young to** drive.*
- *The box is **too heavy for** me **to** lift.*
- *It was **too late to** apologize.*

**Too + adjective + for + person + to + verb**:

- *The coffee was **too hot for** her **to** drink.*

### ...enough to

- *He is **old enough to** vote.*
- *She is **tall enough to** reach the shelf.*
- *I don't have **enough money to** buy it.*

**Adjective/adverb + enough**:

- *The room is **big enough**.*
- *He didn't run **fast enough**.*

**Enough + noun**:

- *We don't have **enough time**.*
- *Do you have **enough food**?*

### So vs such

| | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| so | adjective/adverb alone | so tall, so quickly |
| so | much/many/little/few + noun | so much money |
| such | a/an + adjective + noun | such a beautiful day |
| such | adjective + plural noun | such beautiful days |
| such | adjective + uncountable noun | such beautiful weather |

## Chinese

### <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby>...<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (too...)

<ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby> + adjective + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> expresses excess:

- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>贵<rt>guì</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Too expensive.)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>累<rt>lèi</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Too tired.)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>远<rt>yuǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Too far.)

**Result**: Add consequence with <ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>至<rt>zhì</rt></ruby><ruby>于<rt>yú</rt></ruby> or context:

- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>贵<rt>guì</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby>。(Too expensive, I can't afford it.)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>累<rt>lèi</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>，<ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>至<rt>zhì</rt></ruby><ruby>于<rt>yú</rt></ruby><ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(So tired that I fell asleep.)

### <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>/<ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>... (so...)

<ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby> (this/so) and <ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby> (that/so):

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (so good)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby> (so fast)
- <ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>贵<rt>guì</rt></ruby> (that expensive / so expensive)

**With <ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>至<rt>zhì</rt></ruby><ruby>于<rt>yú</rt></ruby>**:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby>，<ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>至<rt>zhì</rt></ruby><ruby>于<rt>yú</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>追<rt>zhuī</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>。(He runs so fast that I can't catch up.)

### <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>/<ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby><ruby>多<rt>duō</rt></ruby> (so much/many)

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>多<rt>duō</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (so much money)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>多<rt>duō</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby> (so many people)
- <ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>少<rt>shǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>间<rt>jiān</rt></ruby> (so little time)

### <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>能<rt>néng</rt></ruby>/<ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>办<rt>bàn</rt></ruby><ruby>法<rt>fǎ</rt></ruby>... (too...to)

Chinese expresses "too...to" through negative ability:

- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(Too tall, I can't reach.)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>难<rt>nán</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>liǎo</rt></ruby>。(Too hard, I can't do it.)

### <ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby>... (enough)

<ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby> + adjective:

- <ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby> (tall enough)
- <ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (good enough)
- <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (not enough money)

**Pattern**: <ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby> + adjective + <ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby>/<ruby>能<rt>néng</rt></ruby> + verb:

- <ruby>够<rt>gòu</rt></ruby><ruby>大<rt>dà</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>，<ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby>。(Big enough, can enter.)

### <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> (de) for degree result

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>累<rt>lèi</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He was so tired that he fell asleep.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>饿<rt>è</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>能<rt>néng</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>头<rt>tóu</rt></ruby><ruby>牛<rt>niú</rt></ruby>。(I'm so hungry I could eat a horse.)

## Common mistakes

1. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Using very instead of so: *He was very tired that he slept* → *He was **so** tired **that** he slept*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Forgetting agreement in tanto: *tanto dinero* → correct, but *tanta agua* → must agree

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Omitting <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>贵<rt>guì</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby>**<ruby>贵<rt>guì</rt></ruby>**<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>*

4. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Using such with adjective alone: *He is such tall* → *He is **so** tall*
