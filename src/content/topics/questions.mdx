## Overview

Questions are how we request information or confirmation. Every language has its own strategy for turning a statement into a question.

- **Spanish**: Inverts subject and verb for yes/no questions, keeps wh- words at the front, and wraps the whole sentence in inverted question marks `¿...?`.
- **English**: Requires the auxiliary verb *do/does/did* for most questions, except with *be* and modal verbs.
- **Chinese**: Keeps normal word order entirely. Adds a particle (<ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>) or a question word (<ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>, <ruby>谁<rt>shuí</rt></ruby>, <ruby>哪<rt>nǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby>) at the end.

## Spanish

### Yes/no questions

Spanish inverts the subject and verb, or simply changes intonation:

- *Tú vienes.* (You come.) → *¿Vienes (tú)?* (Are you coming?)
- *Ella habla español.* (She speaks Spanish.) → *¿Habla ella español?* (Does she speak Spanish?)

The opening `¿` is **mandatory** in written Spanish. The closing `?` is also required.

### Wh-questions

Question words stay at the front, and the verb usually follows immediately:

| Question word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| qué | what | *¿Qué quieres?* |
| quién | who | *¿Quién es?* |
| dónde | where | *¿Dónde vives?* |
| cuándo | when | *¿Cuándo llegas?* |
| por qué | why | *¿Por qué estudias?* |
| cuánto | how much | *¿Cuánto cuesta?* |

**Important**: Prepositions go before the question word in Spanish: *¿Con quién hablas?* (Who are you talking **to**?), not *¿Quién hablas con?*.

### Tag questions

Spanish uses short phrases at the end of a statement:

- *Vienes, ¿verdad?* (You're coming, right?)
- *No llueve, ¿no?* (It's not raining, is it?)

## English

### Yes/no questions with "do"

For most verbs, English requires the auxiliary *do/does/did*:

- *You want coffee.* → *Do you want coffee?*
- *She speaks Spanish.* → *Does she speak Spanish?*
- *They arrived early.* → *Did they arrive early?*

**Exceptions** — no "do" needed with *be* or modals:

- *She is happy.* → *Is she happy?*
- *They can swim.* → *Can they swim?*

### Wh-questions

The question word comes first, then the auxiliary, then the subject:

| Question word | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| What | What + do/does + subject + verb | *What do you want?* |
| Who | Who + verb (3rd person) | *Who knows?* |
| Where | Where + do/does + subject + verb | *Where does she live?* |
| When | When + did + subject + verb | *When did they arrive?* |
| Why | Why + do/does + subject + verb | *Why do you study?* |
| How | How + do/does + subject + verb | *How does it work?* |

### Tag questions

English tag questions match the auxiliary and reverse the polarity:

- *You're coming, **aren't you**?* (positive statement → negative tag)
- *She doesn't like it, **does she**?* (negative statement → positive tag)

## Chinese

Chinese questions are the simplest of the three languages. Word order stays the same.

### Yes/no questions with <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>

Add the particle <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> to the end of a statement:

- <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(You come.) → <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？(Are you coming?)
- <ruby>她<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huan</rt></ruby><ruby>咖<rt>kā</rt></ruby><ruby>啡<rt>fēi</rt></ruby>。(She likes coffee.) → <ruby>她<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huan</rt></ruby><ruby>咖<rt>kā</rt></ruby><ruby>啡<rt>fēi</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？(Does she like coffee?)

### Wh-questions with question words

Replace the unknown part with a question word. Word order stays the same.

| Question word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby> | what | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby>？ |
| <ruby>谁<rt>shéi</rt></ruby> | who | <ruby>谁<rt>shéi</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby>？ |
| <ruby>哪<rt>nǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby> | where | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>住<rt>zhù</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>哪<rt>nǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby>？ |
| <ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hou</rt></ruby> | when | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hou</rt></ruby><ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby>？ |
| <ruby>为<rt>wèi</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby> | why | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wèi</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shén</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>习<rt>xí</rt></ruby>？ |
| <ruby>怎<rt>zěn</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby> | how | <ruby>怎<rt>zěn</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby>？ |

### Choice questions with <ruby>还<rt>huán</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>

Use <ruby>还<rt>hái</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> (or) between the options:

- <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>茶<rt>chá</rt></ruby><ruby>还<rt>hái</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>咖<rt>kā</rt></ruby><ruby>啡<rt>fēi</rt></ruby>？(Do you want tea or coffee?)

### Follow-up with <ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>

<ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby> turns a statement into a soft follow-up question:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>，<ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>？(I'm fine, and you?)

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Word order for yes/no | Invert subject-verb | Auxiliary + subject + verb | Same as statement |
| Question marks | ¿ ... ? (both ends) | ... ? (end only) | ？ (end only, optional) |
| Auxiliary verb | No auxiliary | do/does/did required | No auxiliary |
| Question particle | None | None | <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> for yes/no |
| Wh- word position | Front (before verb) | Front (before auxiliary) | Replaces the unknown element |
| Preposition with wh- | Before question word | At the end | Before question word |
| Rising intonation | Possible | Very common | Often sufficient |
| Tag question | ¿verdad? / ¿no? | auxiliary + pronoun | <ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby> / <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> |

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish/Chinese speakers learning English**: Forgetting *do/does/did*: *What you want?* → *What do you want?*

2. **English/Spanish speakers learning Chinese**: Changing word order: *<ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？* → *<ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？* (Keep normal order!)

3. **English/Chinese speakers learning Spanish**: Omitting the opening ¿: *Qué pasa?* → *¿Qué pasa?*

4. **English/Spanish speakers learning Chinese**: Using <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> with wh- words: *<ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？* → *<ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>？* (<ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> is only for yes/no)
