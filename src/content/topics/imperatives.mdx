## Overview

Imperatives express commands, requests, instructions, and suggestions.

- **Spanish**: Fully conjugated with distinct forms for each person. Positive and negative forms differ (indicative vs subjunctive). Object pronouns attach to positive imperatives.
- **English**: Uses the base form of the verb for all persons. Negation with *don't*. Very simple but relies heavily on intonation and politeness markers.
- **Chinese**: Uses the verb directly. Particles (<ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby>, <ruby>啊<rt>ā</rt></ruby>, <ruby>嘛<rt>má</rt></ruby>) soften or strengthen the tone. <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby> negates. No person or tense marking.

## Spanish

### Person-specific forms

Spanish imperatives change for each person:

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| tú (informal sing) | ¡**Ven**! | ¡**No vengas**! |
| usted (formal sing) | ¡**Venga**! | ¡**No venga**! |
| vosotros (informal pl Spain) | ¡**Venid**! | ¡**No vengáis**! |
| ustedes (formal pl / Latin Am) | ¡**Vengan**! | ¡**No vengan**! |

**Rule**: Negative imperatives use the **subjunctive**.

### Common irregular imperatives

| Verb | tú | usted | vosotros | ustedes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| venir | ven | venga | venid | vengan |
| ir | ve | vaya | id | vayan |
| ser | sé | sea | sed | sean |
| tener | ten | tenga | tened | tengan |
| hacer | haz | haga | haced | hagan |
| decir | di | diga | decid | digan |
| poner | pon | ponga | poned | pongan |
| salir | sal | salga | salid | salgan |

### Object pronouns with imperatives

In **positive** imperatives, pronouns attach to the verb:

- *¡**Dime**!* (Tell me!)
- *¡**Cuéntamelo**!* (Tell it to me!)
- *¡**Siéntate**!* (Sit down!)

In **negative** imperatives, pronouns precede the verb:

- *¡**No me digas**!* (Don't tell me!)
- *¡**No te sientes**!* (Don't sit down!)

### Politeness strategies

- *¡**Pase**!* (Come in! — formal)
- *¡**Pasa**!* (Come in! — informal)
- *Venga, **por favor**.* (Please come.)
- *Haga el favor de **esperar**.* (Please wait.)

## English

### Base form for all

English uses the **base form** for everyone:

- *Come!*
- *Sit down!*
- *Listen!*
- *Be quiet!*

### Negative

Add *don't* (do not):

- *Don't come!*
- *Don't be late!*
- *Don't worry!*

### Let's for inclusive

*Let's* (let us) includes the speaker:

- *Let's go!*
- *Let's eat!*
- *Let's not argue.* (negative)

### Softening

English softens imperatives with various strategies:

| Strategy | Example |
|---|---|
| Please | *Please sit down.* |
| Question form | *Would you sit down?* |
| Suggestion | *Why don't you sit down?* |
| Softener | *Just sit down.* / *Sit down, will you?* |
| Polite formula | *Would you mind sitting down?* |

### Imperative with subject

English can add a subject for emphasis or clarification:

- *You sit here, and **you** sit there.*
- *Somebody call a doctor!*
- *Don't **you** dare!*

## Chinese

### Bare imperatives

Chinese imperatives use the verb directly:

- <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>！(Come!)
- <ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby>！(Sit!)
- <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby>！(Listen!)
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>！(Go!)

### Softening with <ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby>

<ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby> softens commands into suggestions:

- <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Come on! / Let's come.)
- <ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Have a seat.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>men</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Let's go!)

### Other particles

| Particle | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>啊<rt>a</rt></ruby> | Friendly, urging | <ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>啊<rt>a</rt></ruby>！(Hurry and come!) |
| <ruby>嘛<rt>ma</rt></ruby> | Persuasive | <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>嘛<rt>ma</rt></ruby>！(Come on, please!) |
| <ruby>呀<rt>ya</rt></ruby> | Exclamatory | <ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>呀<rt>ya</rt></ruby>！(Hurry up!) |

### Negative: <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby>

<ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby> negates imperatives:

- <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>！(Don't come!)
- <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby><ruby>担<rt>dān</rt></ruby><ruby>心<rt>xīn</rt></ruby>！(Don't worry!)
- <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>！(Don't go!)

### <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby> as stronger negative

<ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby> is more emphatic:

- <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>碰<rt>pèng</rt></ruby>！(Don't touch!)
- <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>忘<rt>wàng</rt></ruby><ruby>记<rt>jì</rt></ruby>！(Don't forget!)

### <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby> for polite requests

<ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby> (please) precedes the verb:

- <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby><ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby>。(Please sit.)
- <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby><ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby>。(Please come in.)
- <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby><ruby>问<rt>wèn</rt></ruby>...(Excuse me, may I ask...)

### Let's: <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>mén</rt></ruby>...<ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby>

Chinese uses <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>mén</rt></ruby> + verb + <ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby> for inclusive suggestions:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>men</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Let's eat!)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>men</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Let's go!)

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Form | Conjugated per person | Base form (all persons) | Verb directly |
| Negative | subjunctive form | don't + verb | <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby> / <ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby> + verb |
| Positive vs negative | Different forms | Same + don't | Same + <ruby>别<rt>bié</rt></ruby> |
| Politeness | pronoun choice (tú/usted) | please / question form | <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby> / <ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby> / <ruby>啊<rt>ā</rt></ruby> |
| Inclusive let's | Vamos a / Vamos | Let's + verb | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>mén</rt></ruby> + verb + <ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby> |
| Pronoun placement | Attached (pos) / before (neg) | Before verb | Before verb |
| Irregular forms | Many (ven, di, haz, pon...) | Be (be careful) | None |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using base form for all: *venir* → *¡**Ven**!*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Positive form for negative: *No ven* → *No **vengas***

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Adding 'do': *<ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>*

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Omitting subject in emphatic commands: *Sit here* (ambiguous) → *You sit here, and she sits there.*
