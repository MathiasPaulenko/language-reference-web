## Overview

Hedges and mitigation soften utterances, express uncertainty, and maintain politeness.

- **Spanish**: Uses the subjunctive mood to encode doubt. Diminutives (*-ito*) soften nouns. Periphrastic constructions (*más o menos, a lo mejor*) express vagueness.
- **English**: Extensive hedge system. Modal verbs (*might, could*), adverbs (*maybe, probably*), approximators (*kind of, sort of*), and politeness formulas (*I was wondering if...*).
- **Chinese**: Adverbs and particles do the hedging work. <ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby><ruby>能<rt>néng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>大<rt>dà</rt></ruby><ruby>概<rt>gài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby><ruby>许<rt>xǔ</rt></ruby> for epistemic uncertainty. <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby>, <ruby>稍<rt>shāo</rt></ruby><ruby>微<rt>wēi</rt></ruby> for degree. <ruby>一<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> for action softening. No grammatical mood like the subjunctive.

## Spanish

### Epistemic hedges (doubt/uncertainty)

| Hedge | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| quizá(s) | perhaps | *Quizá llueva.* |
| tal vez | perhaps | *Tal vez venga.* |
| a lo mejor | maybe | *A lo mejor no.* |
| probablemente | probably | *Probablemente sí.* |
| posiblemente | possibly | *Posiblemente lo sepa.* |
| puede que | it may be that | *Puede que tengas razón.* |
| igual | maybe (Spain) | *Igual no viene.* |

**Note**: These triggers require the subjunctive when referring to future or uncertain events.

### Diminutive hedges

Adding *-ito/-ita* softens and makes friendly:

- *un momentito* (just a moment)
- *un poquito* (a tiny bit)
- *rapidito* (real quick)
- *ahorita* (in a little while — Latin America)
- *esperame un ratito* (wait just a bit for me)

### Approximators

- *más o menos* (more or less)
- *aproximadamente* (approximately)
- *alrededor de* (around)
- *unos* (about — before numbers)
- *casi* (almost)
- *prácticamente* (practically)

### Modal hedges

- *poder* (can/could): *¿Podrías ayudarme?*
- *deber* (should/ought): *Deberías estudiar.*
- *tener que* (have to): *Tengo que irme.*

### Politeness formulas

- *¿Te importaría...?* (Would you mind...?)
- *¿Podrías...?* (Could you...?)
- *Me gustaría...* (I would like...)
- *¿Sería posible...?* (Would it be possible...?)
- *Ojalá...* (I hope... — requires subjunctive)

### Vague responses

- *No sé...* (I don't know...)
- *Es complicado...* (It's complicated...)
- *A ver...* (Let's see...)
- *Depende...* (It depends...)
- *Ya veremos...* (We'll see...)

## English

### Epistemic hedges

| Hedge | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|
| definitely | certain | *It definitely is.* |
| probably | likely | *It probably is.* |
| maybe / perhaps | possible | *Maybe it is.* |
| possibly | weak possibility | *It possibly is.* |
| not sure | uncertain | *I'm not sure.* |

### Approximators

- *about / around* (numbers): *about ten people*
- *roughly / approximately* (formal): *approximately 50%*
- *kind of / sort of* (quality): *kind of tired*
- *somewhat* (formal): *somewhat difficult*
- *more or less* (Spanish loan): *more or less finished*

### Modal hedges

| Modal | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| might | weak possibility | *It might rain.* |
| could | possibility/polite request | *Could you help?* |
| would | hypothetical | *I would say...* |
| should | expectation | *It should work.* |
| may | permission/possibility | *You may be right.* |

### Politeness formulas

- *I was wondering if...*
- *I was hoping to...*
- *Do you think you might...?*
- *Would it be possible to...?*
- *If you don't mind, could you...?*
- *I'm sorry to bother you, but...*

### Vague language

- *thing* / *stuff* / *whatever*
- *something like that*
- *or something*
- *you know what I mean*
- *and so on / etcetera*

### Hedged performatives

- *I guess I'll go.* (not I will go)
- *I suppose so.* (not Yes)
- *I tend to think...* (not I think)
- *If I may say so...*
- *If you ask me...* (but you didn't)

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Underusing hedges: *Do you help me?* → *Could you help me?*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using indicative after quizá: *Quizá viene* → *Quizá **venga***

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Using <ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby> for all intensification: *<ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>* is neutral; for hedge use *<ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>* or *<ruby>比<rt>bǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>较<rt>jiào</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>*

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Direct negatives without softening: *I don't like it* → *I'm not sure I like it* / *I don't really like it*
