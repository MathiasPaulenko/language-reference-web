## Overview

Wishes and hypotheticals express desires for unreal or uncertain situations.

- **Spanish**: Uses *ojalá* (from Arabic *inshallah*) + subjunctive. The conditional expresses hypothetical results. The imperfect and pluperfect subjunctive are essential.
- **English**: Uses *wish* + past tense for present counterfactuals, *wish* + past perfect for past counterfactuals, and *wish* + would for future/behavioral desires. *Hope* is for real possibilities.
- **Chinese**: Has no grammatical subjunctive or backshift. Uses <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>/<ruby>如<rt>rú</rt></ruby><ruby>果<rt>guǒ</rt></ruby> for hypotheticals, <ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby> for wishes, and particles (<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>, <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>) to mark counterfactuality.

## Spanish

### Ojalá + subjunctive

*Ojalá* expresses a strong wish and always triggers the subjunctive:

**Present / future wish**:

- *Ojalá **venga** mañana.* (I hope he comes tomorrow.)
- *Ojalá **llueva**.* (I hope it rains.)
- *Ojalá **tengas** razón.* (I hope you're right.)

**Past counterfactual**:

- *Ojalá **hubiera venido**.* (If only he had come.)
- *Ojalá **hubiera estudiado** más.* (If only I had studied more.)

**Imperfect counterfactual** (ongoing/background):

- *Ojalá **fuera** más fácil.* (If only it were easier.)
- *Ojalá **pudiera** ayudarte.* (If only I could help you.)

### Esperar que + subjunctive

*Esperar* (hope) also triggers subjunctive:

- *Espero que **venga**.* (I hope he comes.)
- *Espero que **estés** bien.* (I hope you're well.)
- *Esperaba que **hubiera venido**.* (I hoped he had come.)

### Querer que + subjunctive

- *Quiero que **vengas**.* (I want you to come.)
- *Quería que **vinieras**.* (I wanted you to come.)

### Preferir / Más vale + subjunctive

- *Prefiero que **no vengas**.* (I'd rather you didn't come.)
- *Más vale que **te vayas**.* (You'd better go.)

### Si + subjunctive (contrary to fact)

- *Si **tuviera** dinero, viajaría.* (If I had money, I would travel.)
- *Si **hubiera estudiado**, habría aprobado.* (If I had studied, I would have passed.)

### Hypothetical expressions

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| como si + subjunctive | as if | *Habla como si **supiera** todo.* |
| aunque + subjunctive | even if | *Aunque **venga**, no importa.* |
| sin que + subjunctive | without | *Se fue sin que **lo viera**.* |
| para que + subjunctive | so that | *Ven para que **hablemos**.* |

## English

### Wish + past (present counterfactual)

*Wish* expresses a desire for a situation that is not true now:

- *I wish I **were** richer.* (I'm not rich.)
- *I wish I **knew** the answer.* (I don't know.)
- *I wish she **lived** closer.* (She doesn't live close.)
- *I wish it **weren't** raining.* (It is raining.)

**Were** for all persons (formal):

- *I wish I **were** taller.* (preferred in formal)
- *I wish I **was** taller.* (acceptable in informal)

### Wish + past perfect (past counterfactual)

- *I wish I **had studied** harder.* (I didn't study hard.)
- *I wish she **had come**.* (She didn't come.)
- *I wish I **hadn't said** that.* (I said it.)

### Wish + would (future/behavioral)

- *I wish he **would stop** talking.* (He keeps talking.)
- *I wish it **would rain**.* (It hasn't rained.)
- *I wish you **would listen**.* (You don't listen.)

**Cannot** use would with first person for self-wishes:

- ❌ *I wish I would be taller.* → *I wish I **were** taller.*

### If only

*If only* is stronger than *wish*:

- *If only I **knew**!*
- *If only I **had listened**!*
- *If only he **would leave**!*

### Hope (real possibilities)

- *I **hope** he **comes**.* (Possible — indicative)
- *I **hope** it **doesn't rain**.* (Possible — indicative)

**Never** use hope for counterfactuals:

- ❌ *I hope I were rich.* → *I **wish** I were rich.*

### Would rather

- *I'd rather you **didn't smoke**.* (past = present/future preference)
- *I'd rather you **hadn't told** him.* (past perfect = past preference)

**It's time** (urging action):

- *It's time we **left**.* (past = present obligation)
- *It's high time he **grew** up.*

### Supposing / What if

- *Supposing he **refused**?*
- *What if it **rained**?*

## Chinese

### <ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby> (hope — real possibility)

<ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby> expresses hope for real possibilities:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby><ruby>明<rt>míng</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby>。(I hope it doesn't rain tomorrow.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby><ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>能<rt>néng</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(I hope you can come.)

### <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>/<ruby>如<rt>rú</rt></ruby><ruby>果<rt>guǒ</rt></ruby> + <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (wish/counterfactual)

<ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> = "If only..."

- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(If only I had money.)
- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(If only he had come.)
- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>当<rt>dāng</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(If only I had known.)

### <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> + question word (what if)

- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby><ruby>怎<rt>zěn</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>me</rt></ruby><ruby>办<rt>bàn</rt></ruby>？(What if it rains?)
- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>？(What if he doesn't come?)

### <ruby>宁<rt>níng</rt></ruby><ruby>愿<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby> / <ruby>宁<rt>níng</rt></ruby><ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby> (would rather)

<ruby>宁<rt>nìng</rt></ruby><ruby>愿<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby> / <ruby>宁<rt>nìng</rt></ruby><ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby> = would rather:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>宁<rt>nìng</rt></ruby><ruby>愿<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>家<rt>jiā</rt></ruby>。(I'd rather stay home.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>宁<rt>nìng</rt></ruby><ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby><ruby>死<rt>sǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby>。(I'd rather die than go.)

### <ruby>早<rt>zǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>... (if I had known)

<ruby>早<rt>zǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby> = "if I had known earlier":

- <ruby>早<rt>zǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(If I had known, I wouldn't have gone.)

### No tense change

Chinese verbs do not change for hypothetical vs real:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>希<rt>xī</rt></ruby><ruby>望<rt>wàng</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(I hope he comes. — real)
- <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(If only he would come. — counterfactual)

**Same verb form**: <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> in both cases. Context and <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> mark the counterfactual.

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using indicative after ojalá: *Ojalá viene* → *Ojalá **venga***

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Using hope for counterfactuals: *I hope I were rich* → *I **wish** I were rich*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Adding tense backshift: *<ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>曾<rt>zēng</rt></ruby><ruby>经<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>*

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Confusing hope and wish: *I wish he comes* → *I **hope** he comes*
