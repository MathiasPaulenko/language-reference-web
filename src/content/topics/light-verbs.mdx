## Overview

Light verbs are high-frequency, semantically general verbs that combine with nouns to create more specific meanings. The noun carries most of the semantic content.

- **Spanish**: Uses *dar* (give), *hacer* (make/do), *tomar* (take), *tener* (have), *pasar* (spend), and *echar* (throw/look) as light verbs. Many are reflexive.
- **English**: Uses *take, give, have, make, do, get* as the core light verbs. Extremely productive in phrasal combinations.
- **Chinese**: Often uses the noun itself as a verb (<ruby>散<rt>sàn</rt></ruby><ruby>歩<rt>bù</rt></ruby> = to walk). When a light verb is needed, <ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby>, <ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>行<rt>háng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>加<rt>jiā</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby>, or <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby> are used. Reduplication and <ruby>一<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> soften the action.

## Chinese

### Verb-noun as single unit (no light verb)

Chinese often combines noun and verb into a single word:

| Word | Noun | Verb |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>散<rt>sàn</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby> | walk (noun) | walk (verb) |
| <ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby> | swimming (noun) | swim (verb) |
| <ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>觉<rt>jiào</rt></ruby> | sleep (noun) | sleep (verb) |
| <ruby>洗<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>澡<rt>zǎo</rt></ruby> | bath (noun) | bathe (verb) |
| <ruby>唱<rt>chàng</rt></ruby><ruby>歌<rt>gē</rt></ruby> | song (noun) | sing (verb) |
| <ruby>跳<rt>tiào</rt></ruby><ruby>舞<rt>wǔ</rt></ruby> | dance (noun) | dance (verb) |
| <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby> | meal (noun) | eat (verb) |
| <ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>觉<rt>jiào</rt></ruby> | sleep (noun) | sleep (verb) |

**No light verb needed**: <ruby>散<rt>sàn</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby> already means "take a walk."

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Tomar una foto* is Latin American; Spain prefers *hacer una foto*

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Do a decision* → *make a decision*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>散<rt>sàn</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>散<rt>sàn</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby>* (no light verb needed)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *I will walk-walk* → *I'll take a walk* / *I'll go for a walk*
