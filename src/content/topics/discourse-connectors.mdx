## Overview

Discourse connectors organize text above the sentence level, showing how ideas relate.

- **Spanish**: Rich Latinate connector inventory. Many are multi-word phrases (*por lo tanto, a pesar de eso*). Register variation is significant (formal *no obstante* vs colloquial *o sea*).
- **English**: Mix of Germanic and Latinate connectors. Academic style prefers single-word Latinate forms (*however, therefore*) over phrasal Germanic ones (*even so, because of that*).
- **Chinese**: Connectors can be explicit (<ruby>而<rt>ér</rt></ruby><ruby>且<rt>qiě</rt></ruby>, <ruby>然<rt>rán</rt></ruby><ruby>而<rt>ér</rt></ruby>, <ruby>因<rt>yīn</rt></ruby><ruby>此<rt>cǐ</rt></ruby>) or implicit (topic continuity, zero anaphora). Paired structures (<ruby>虽<rt>suī</rt></ruby><ruby>然<rt>rán</rt></ruby>...<ruby>但<rt>dàn</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>, <ruby>因<rt>yīn</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby>...<ruby>所<rt>suǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby>) are common.

### Implicit cohesion

Chinese often omits connectors when topic continuity is clear:

- <ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby>。<ruby>今<rt>jīn</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby>。(It rained yesterday. [And] today it's raining too. — connector omitted)

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Literal translation: *Por lo tanto* → *For the so* → *Therefore*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Omitting commas around connector: *Sin embargo no es cierto* → *Sin embargo**,** no es cierto*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Overusing connectors: Chinese often omits them when topic is continuous

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Using informal connectors in academic writing: *So, we must act* → *Therefore, we must act*
