## Overview

Interjections are exclamatory words that express emotion independently of sentence grammar.

- **Spanish**: Uses ¡! around interjections. Many interjections are shared with or borrowed from English (ouch, wow). Regional variation is rich.
- **English**: Highly productive interjection system. Includes primary interjections (ouch, wow, shh) and secondary interjections derived from other word classes (damn, heavens, goodness).
- **Chinese**: Uses interjection characters that are also tonal. Often combines with sentence-final particles (<ruby>啊<rt>ā</rt></ruby>, <ruby>呀<rt>yā</rt></ruby>, <ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>) to express nuanced emotion.

## Spanish

### Primary interjections

| Interjection | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ¡Ay! | pain, regret | ¡Ay! Me duele. |
| ¡Oh! | surprise | ¡Oh! No lo sabía. |
| ¡Eh! | attention, hey | ¡Eh! Tú, ven aquí. |
| ¡Vaya! | surprise | ¡Vaya! Qué sorpresa. |
| ¡Ojo! | watch out | ¡Ojo! con el coche. |
| ¡Bravo! | approval | ¡Bravo! Muy bien. |
| ¡Olé! | celebration | ¡Olé! por el torero. |
| ¡Hurra! | celebration | ¡Hurra! Ganamos. |
| ¡Bah! | dismissal | ¡Bah! No me importa. |
| ¡Puf! | effort/exhaustion | ¡Puf! Qué calor. |
| ¡Chss! | silence | ¡Chss! Estoy escuchando. |
| ¡Puaj! / ¡Ugh! | disgust | ¡Puaj! Qué asco. |

### Secondary interjections

Words from other categories used as interjections:

- ***¡Dios!*** (God!)
- ***¡Cielos!*** (Heavens!)
- ***¡Diablos!*** (Devils! — dammit)
- ***¡Vamos!*** (Come on!)
- ***¡Anda!*** (Go on! / Really!)
- ***¡Hombre!*** (Man! / Come on!)
- ***¡Caray!*** (Gosh!)
- ***¡Caramba!*** (Goodness!)

### Exclamatory phrases

- ***¡Qué bonito!*** (How beautiful!)
- ***¡Qué lástima!*** (What a shame!)
- ***¡Qué horror!*** (How awful!)

### Borrowed interjections

- ***¡Wow!*** / ***¡Guau!***
- ***¡Oops!*** / ***¡Uy!***
- ***¡Yeah!***

### Interjection + sentence-final particle

Chinese often combines interjections with particles:

- <ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>棒<rt>bàng</rt></ruby><ruby>啊<rt>a</rt></ruby>！ (Great! — <ruby>啊<rt>ā</rt></ruby> softens/amplifies)
- <ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>呀<rt>ya</rt></ruby>！ (Really good!)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>！ (Great! — <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> marks change)

### Exclamatory phrases

- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>！ (Wonderful!)
- <ruby>可<rt>kě</rt></ruby><ruby>惜<rt>xī</rt></ruby>！ (What a pity!)
- <ruby>厉<rt>lì</rt></ruby><ruby>害<rt>hài</rt></ruby>！ (Awesome!)
- <ruby>万<rt>wàn</rt></ruby><ruby>岁<rt>suì</rt></ruby>！ (Hooray!)

### Borrowed interjections

- **<ruby>哇<rt>wā</rt></ruby><ruby>塞<rt>sāi</rt></ruby>** (wāsài) — from Taiwanese Hokkien, "wow"
- **<ruby>哦<rt>ó</rt></ruby><ruby>耶<rt>yé</rt></ruby>** (ōyé) — "oh yeah"
- **<ruby>耶<rt>yé</rt></ruby>** (yé) — "yay"

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Ouch, me duele* → *¡**Ay**! Me duele.*

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Ay! That hurt!* → ***Ouch**! That hurt!*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>哇<rt>wā</rt></ruby>* without tone → <ruby>哇<rt>wā</rt></ruby> (high tone required)

4. **Chinese speakers learning Spanish**: *<ruby>哦<rt>ó</rt></ruby>* → *¡**Oh**!* (not *¡O* without closing mark)
