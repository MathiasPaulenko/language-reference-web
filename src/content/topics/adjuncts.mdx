## Overview

Adjuncts are optional modifiers that add circumstantial information to a sentence.

- **Spanish**: Uses adverbs (*-mente*), prepositional phrases (*en, a, con, por, para*), and clauses. Position is relatively flexible.
- **English**: Uses adverbs (*-ly*), prepositional phrases, and clauses. Position varies by adjunct type.
- **Chinese**: Uses adverbial phrases (<ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby>...<ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>), coverb phrases (<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby>, <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby>), and clauses (<ruby>因<rt>yīn</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby>...<ruby>所<rt>suǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby>). Time adjuncts typically precede the verb.

## Spanish

### Time adjuncts

| Type | Expression | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Specific time | a las tres | *Llegó a las tres.* |
| Duration | durante dos horas | *Estudió durante dos horas.* |
| Frequency | todos los días | *Viene todos los días.* |
| Point in time | ayer, mañana | *Llegó ayer.* |

### Place adjuncts

| Preposition | Usage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| en | in/at (countries, cities) | *en Madrid, en España* |
| a | to (direction) | *va a Madrid* |
| de | from | *es de Madrid* |
| desde | from (starting point) | *desde aquí* |
| hasta | until/to | *hasta mañana* |

### Manner adjuncts

- *rápid**amente*** (quickly)
- *cuidados**amente*** (carefully)
- *bien* (well)
- *mal* (badly)
- *así* (like this)
- *de prisa* (in a hurry)
- *con cuidado* (with care)

### Reason/cause

- *Llegó tarde **por** la lluvia.* (because of the rain)
- *No vino **a causa del** tráfico.* (due to traffic)
- **Por** vs **para**: *por* = cause/means; *para* = purpose/destination

### Instrument/means

- *Viajó **en** coche.* (by car)
- *Vino **a pie**.* (on foot)
- *Lo escribió **con** lápiz.* (with pencil)

### Accompaniment

- *Vino **con** María.* (with María)

## English

### Time adjuncts

| Type | Expression | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Specific time | at 3 o'clock | *He arrived at 3.* |
| Duration | for two hours | *He stayed for two hours.* |
| Frequency | every day | *He comes every day.* |
| Point in time | yesterday, tomorrow | *He arrived yesterday.* |

**Note**: *For* + duration, *since* + starting point, *ago* + past time.

### Place adjuncts

| Preposition | Usage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| in | large spaces, enclosed | *in London, in the room* |
| at | specific points | *at the station, at home* |
| on | surfaces | *on the table, on the street* |
| to | direction | *to London* |
| from | origin | *from London* |
| into | entering | *into the room* |
| out of | exiting | *out of the room* |

### Manner adjuncts

- *quick**ly*** (quick + -ly)
- *careful**ly*** (careful + -ly)
- *well* (irregular)
- *badly* (irregular)
- *fast* (adjective = adverb)
- *hard* (adjective = adverb)
- *in a hurry*
- *with care*

### Reason/cause

- *He arrived late **because of** the rain.*
- *He stayed home **due to** illness.*
- ***Because** it rained, he stayed.* (clause)

### Instrument/means

- *He came **by** car.*
- *He came **on** foot.*
- *He wrote it **with** a pencil.*

### Accompaniment

- *He came **with** Maria.*

## Chinese

### Time adjuncts

Time typically precedes the subject or verb:

- <ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Yesterday he came.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He yesterday came. — also acceptable)

| Type | Expression | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Specific time | <ruby>三<rt>sān</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>钟<rt>zhōng</rt></ruby> | <ruby>三<rt>sān</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>钟<rt>zhōng</rt></ruby> |

### Place adjuncts

Coverb <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> for location:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>。(He is in Beijing.)

### Manner adjuncts

- <ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby> (run quickly — <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> marks adverb)
- <ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>man</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (walk slowly)

### Reason/cause

<ruby>因<rt>yīn</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby>...<ruby>所<rt>suǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby>:
- <ruby>因<rt>yīn</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wèi</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby>，<ruby>所<rt>suǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>迟<rt>chí</rt></ruby><ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。

### Instrument/means

- <ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>车<rt>chē</rt></ruby> (by car — <ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby> = sit/ride)
- <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby><ruby>笔<rt>bǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>写<rt>xiě</rt></ruby> (write with pen — <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby> = use)

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Llegó para la lluvia* → *Llegó **por** la lluvia* (por = cause)

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *He arrived quicklyly* → *He arrived **quickly*** (just one -ly)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby>**<ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby>**<ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* (time before verb)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *He by car came* → *He came **by car*** (English PP follows verb)
