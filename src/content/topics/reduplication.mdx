## Overview

Reduplication is the repetition of a word or part of a word for grammatical or expressive purposes.

- **Spanish**: Minimal reduplication. Occasionally used colloquially for emphasis (*muy muy*). Diminutives (*-ito*) function similarly to reduplication in expressing affection or smallness.
- **English**: Very limited reduplication. Used in child-directed speech (*choo-choo, wee-wee*), some compounds (*tick-tock, zig-zag*), and informal intensification (*so-so, goody-goody*).
- **Chinese**: Extensive and grammatically productive reduplication system. Verbs, adjectives, measure words, and nouns can all be reduplicated with distinct meanings.

## Spanish

### No grammatical reduplication

Spanish does not use reduplication as a grammatical device. Repetition is either:

1. **Accidental** (stutter, emphasis)
2. **Colloquial** (very informal)
3. **Lexicalized** (fixed expressions)

### Colloquial emphasis

- *muy muy* (very very — childish or playful)
- *mucho mucho* (a lot a lot)
- *ya ya* (yeah yeah — dismissive)

### Lexicalized reduplication

A few words exist in reduplicated form:

- *boni-boni* (very nice — regional, childish)
- *pispis* (pee-pee — childish)
- *pipi* (pee-pee — childish)

### Diminutives as functional equivalent

Spanish uses diminutives where Chinese might use reduplication:

- *un momentito* (a little moment = just a sec)
- *un poquito* (a little bit)
- *espera un ratito* (wait a little while)
- *ven aquí, mi amorcito* (come here, my little love = term of affection)

### Periphrasis instead of reduplication

Where Chinese reduplicates verbs to soften, Spanish uses periphrastic constructions:

- *echar un vistazo* (take a look)
- *dar un paseo* (take a walk)
- *tomar un café* (have a coffee)
- *hacer una llamada* (make a call)

## English

### Very limited reduplication

English reduplication is mostly:

1. **Child-directed speech**
2. **Onomatopoeia**
3. **Informal emphasis**
4. **Ablaut reduplication** (vowel change)

### Child-directed speech

| Reduplicated | Meaning |
|---|---|
| choo-choo | train |
| wee-wee | urination |
| poo-poo | defecation |
| night-night | goodnight |
| bye-bye | goodbye |
| yum-yum | delicious |
| teddy bear (from Edward) | stuffed toy |

### Ablaut reduplication (vowel change)

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| tick-tock | clock sound |
| zig-zag | back and forth |
| flip-flop | sandals / reversal |
| ticky-tacky | cheap, uniform |
| mish-mash | confused mixture |
| riff-raff | disreputable people |
| tip-top | excellent |
| wishy-washy | indecisive |
| hocus-pocus | magic |
| helter-skelter | disorderly |

**Rule**: For ablaut reduplication, the vowel order is I-A-O:
- *tic**k**-t**a**c**k***
- *zig-**z**ag*
- *cr**i**sp-**c**r**a**ck**l**e*

### Informal/full reduplication

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| so-so | mediocre |
| goody-goody | self-righteous |
| go-go | active, energetic |
| rah-rah | enthusiastic |
| no-no | something forbidden |
| ho-ho | Santa's laugh |

### No grammatical verb reduplication

English does **not** reduplicate verbs grammatically:

- ❌ *Look-look at this.* → *Take a look at this.*
- ❌ *Try-try it.* → *Give it a try.*

## Chinese

### Verb reduplication (VV / V-V / V<ruby>一<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby>)

Reduplicating a verb softens the action, making it tentative or brief:

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| VV | <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kan</rt></ruby> | have a look |
| VV | <ruby>试<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>试<rt>shi</rt></ruby> | give it a try |
| VV | <ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiang</rt></ruby> | think about it |
| VV | <ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>说<rt>shuo</rt></ruby> | talk a bit |
| VV | <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby><ruby>听<rt>ting</rt></ruby> | listen a bit |
| V-V | <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby> | have a look |
| V<ruby>一<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> | <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> | have a look |

**Softening effect**:

- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby>！(Look! — command)
- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kan</rt></ruby>！(Have a look — soft request)
- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby>。(Take a look — polite request)

### Adjective reduplication

**Monosyllabic: AA + <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>**

Softens or intensifies, often with affection:

- <ruby>红<rt>hóng</rt></ruby><ruby>红<rt>hóng</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (nice and red / reddish)
- <ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (slow and easy)
- <ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (nice and tall)

**Disyllabic: AABB**

Emphasizes or describes a state vividly:

- <ruby>漂<rt>piào</rt></ruby><ruby>漂<rt>piào</rt></ruby><ruby>亮<rt>liang</rt></ruby><ruby>亮<rt>liang</rt></ruby> (very pretty)
- <ruby>干<rt>gān</rt></ruby><ruby>干<rt>gān</rt></ruby><ruby>净<rt>jìng</rt></ruby><ruby>净<rt>jìng</rt></ruby> (spotlessly clean)
- <ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>兴<rt>xìng</rt></ruby><ruby>兴<rt>xìng</rt></ruby> (very happy)
- <ruby>马<rt>mǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>马<rt>mǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>虎<rt>hǔ</rt></ruby><ruby>虎<rt>hǔ</rt></ruby> (careless, so-so)

**ABAB (for verbs/adjectives)**

Turns adjectives into verbs of<ruby>尝<rt>cháng</rt></ruby><ruby>试<rt>shì</rt></ruby>/assessment:

- <ruby>舒<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby><ruby>舒<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby> (see if it's comfortable)
- <ruby>漂<rt>piào</rt></ruby><ruby>亮<rt>liang</rt></ruby><ruby>漂<rt>piào</rt></ruby><ruby>亮<rt>liang</rt></ruby> (make it prettier)

### Measure word reduplication

Reduplicating a measure word means "each and every":

- <ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>。(Each and every one is good.)
- <ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby>。(It rains every single day.)
- <ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(Everybody knows.)

### Noun reduplication (limited)

Some nouns can reduplicate for "every":

- <ruby>家<rt>jiā</rt></ruby><ruby>家<rt>jiā</rt></ruby><ruby>户<rt>hù</rt></ruby><ruby>户<rt>hù</rt></ruby> (every household)
- <ruby>年<rt>nián</rt></ruby><ruby>年<rt>nián</rt></ruby><ruby>月<rt>yuè</rt></ruby><ruby>月<rt>yuè</rt></ruby> (every year and month)

### Rhetorical reduplication

- <ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby> (look here — drawing attention)
- <ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby> (step by step)

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Forgetting reduplication for softening: *<ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>* (softer)

2. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Creating reduplicated verbs: *look-look* → *take a look*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Using AABB for all adjectives: *<ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>* is not standard → *<ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>* or *<ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>兴<rt>xīng</rt></ruby><ruby>兴<rt>xīng</rt></ruby>*

4. **Spanish speakers learning Chinese**: Missing the softening function: confusing bare verbs (commands) with reduplicated verbs (requests)
