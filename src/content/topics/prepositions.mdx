## Overview

Prepositions are small words that show relationships — location, time, direction, cause, and more.

- **Spanish**: Fewer prepositions than English, but they combine with articles (*al, del*). Two tricky prepositions (*por* vs *para*) both translate as "for" but have distinct uses.
- **English**: Many prepositions with subtle distinctions. Phrasal verbs (*give up, look after, turn on*) are a major challenge. Preposition stranding is allowed in questions.
- **Chinese**: Uses a small set of core prepositions (<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>从<rt>cóng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby>, <ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby>). Postpositions (<ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby>, <ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby>, <ruby>外<rt>wài</rt></ruby>) often follow nouns to show location.

## Spanish

### Core prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| a | to, at | *Voy **a** casa.* |
| de | of, from | *Soy **de** Madrid.* |
| en | in, on, at | *Estoy **en** casa.* |
| con | with | *Voy **con** María.* |
| por | for, by, through | *Paso **por** aquí.* |
| para | for, to, in order to | *Es **para** ti.* |
| sin | without | *Sin dinero.* |
| sobre | on, about | *Sobre la mesa.* |

### Contractions with articles

- *a + el = **al***: *Voy **al** mercado.* (to the market)
- *de + el = **del***: *Vengo **del** mercado.* (from the market)

No contraction with feminine articles:

- *a la escuela* (to the school)
- *de la escuela* (from the school)

### Por vs para

| | por | para |
|---|---|---|
| Cause/reason | *Lo hice **por** ti.* (for your sake) | — |
| Duration | *Estudié **por** dos horas.* | — |
| Movement through | *Pasamos **por** Madrid.* | — |
| Medium | *Hablamos **por** teléfono.* | — |
| Purpose/goal | — | *Es **para** estudiar.* (in order to study) |
| Destination | — | *Salgo **para** Madrid.* (bound for) |
| Recipient | — | *Es **para** ti.* (intended for) |
| Deadline | — | *Lo necesito **para** mañana.* |

### Preposition before infinitive

Spanish uses prepositions to link verbs:

- *Voy **a** comer.* (I'm going to eat.)
- *Acabo **de** comer.* (I just ate.)
- *Empiezo **a** trabajar.* (I start working.)

### Phrasal verbs

A phrasal verb combines a verb + particle (often a preposition) with an idiomatic meaning:

| Phrasal verb | Literal | Idiomatic | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| give up | give + up | surrender | *Don't **give up**.* |
| look after | look + after | care for | *She **looks after** her sister.* |
| turn on | turn + on | activate | *Please **turn on** the light.* |
| run out of | run + out + of | exhaust | *We **ran out of** milk.* |
| put up with | put + up + with | tolerate | *I can't **put up with** this.* |

**Important**: The particle is not optional — *look after* is a single unit.

### Preposition stranding

English allows prepositions at the end of questions and relative clauses:

- *Who are you talking **to**?* (instead of *To whom are you talking?*)
- *The person I spoke **with**.* (instead of *The person with whom I spoke.*)

**Formal/written style** prefers fronting the preposition. **Informal/spoken style** prefers stranding.

### Postpositions for location

Chinese uses **postpositions** after nouns to show location:

| Postposition | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby> | on, above | <ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby> (on the table) |
| <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> | under, below | <ruby>椅<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby> (under the chair) |
| <ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby> | inside | <ruby>房<rt>fáng</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby> (inside the house) |
| <ruby>外<rt>wài</rt></ruby> | outside | <ruby>门<rt>mén</rt></ruby><ruby>外<rt>wài</rt></ruby> (outside the door) |
| <ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby> | in front of | <ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>校<rt>xiào</rt></ruby><ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (in front of school) |
| <ruby>后<rt>hòu</rt></ruby> | behind | <ruby>车<rt>chē</rt></ruby><ruby>后<rt>hòu</rt></ruby> (behind the car) |
| <ruby>旁<rt>páng</rt></ruby><ruby>边<rt>biān</rt></ruby> | next to | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>旁<rt>páng</rt></ruby><ruby>边<rt>biān</rt></ruby> (next to me) |

### Pattern: <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> + location + postposition

- <ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>。(The book is on the table.)
- <ruby>猫<rt>māo</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>房<rt>fáng</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby>。(The cat is inside the house.)

### No preposition stranding

Chinese does not strand prepositions:

- <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby><ruby>谁<rt>shéi</rt></ruby><ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>话<rt>huà</rt></ruby>？(With whom are you speaking?)
- ❌ <ruby>谁<rt>shéi</rt></ruby><ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>话<rt>huà</rt></ruby><ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby> (ungrammatical)

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Confusing *por* and *para*: *Es por ti* (because of you) vs *Es para ti* (it's for you)

2. **Spanish/Chinese speakers learning English**: Ignoring phrasal verbs: *look the word in the dictionary* → *look **up** the word*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Omitting <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>*

4. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Using prepositions like English: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zǐ</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>*
