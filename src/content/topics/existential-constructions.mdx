## Overview

Existential constructions state that something exists or is present, without identifying a specific location or possessor.

- **Spanish**: Uses *haber* in its impersonal form (*hay, había, hubo*). Invariant third-person singular even with plural subjects. Strictly distinguishes from location (*estar*) and possession (*tener*).
- **English**: Uses the dummy subject *there* with forms of *be*. Agreement is sometimes formal (*there is three* vs *there are three*). Distinguishes from *it is* (identification).
- **Chinese**: Uses *<ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>* for existence, possession, and location. The location typically precedes *<ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>* in existential constructions. Negation uses *<ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>*.

## Spanish

### Hay (present)

- *Hay un libro.* (There is a book.)
- *Hay muchos libros.* (There are many books. — hay remains singular)
- *Hay gente en la calle.* (There are people in the street.)

**Note**: *Hay* is invariant; it does not agree with the noun.

### Había (imperfect — ongoing/state)

- *Había un problema.* (There was a problem. — ongoing)
- *Había muchos libros.* (There were many books.)
- *Había gente en la fiesta.* (There were people at the party.)

### Hubo (preterite — punctual/event)

- *Hubo un accidente.* (There was an accident. — event)
- *Hubo una explosión.* (There was an explosion.)
- *Hubo dos muertos.* (There were two deaths.)

**Hubo vs había**: *Hubo* for sudden, discrete events; *había* for ongoing states.

### Habrá (future)

- *Habrá una reunión mañana.* (There will be a meeting tomorrow.)

### Habría (conditional)

- *Habría un problema.* (There would be a problem.)

### Negation

- *No hay agua.* (There is no water.)
- *No había nadie.* (There was nobody.)
- *No hubo problema.* (There was no problem.)

### With adverbs

- *Todavía hay tiempo.* (There is still time.)
- *Ya no hay excusas.* (There are no more excuses.)
- *¿Todavía hay café?* (Is there still coffee?)

### Distinguishing existence, location, possession

| Construction | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Existence | hay | *Hay un libro.* |
| Location | está | *El libro está en la mesa.* |
| Possession | tiene | *Juan tiene un libro.* |

## English

### There is / there are

- *There **is** a book.* (singular)
- *There **are** many books.* (plural)
- *There **is** some water.* (uncountable)

**Note**: Informally, *there is* is often used with plural subjects:

- *There's three books on the table.* (colloquial)
- *There **are** three books on the table.* (formal)

### There was / there were

- *There **was** a problem.*
- *There **were** many people.*
- *There **was** an accident.*

### There will be / there would be

- *There **will be** a meeting tomorrow.*
- *There **would be** trouble if he came.*
- *There **should be** enough.*

### There seems to be / there appears to be

- *There **seems to be** a problem.*
- *There **appears to be** some confusion.*
- *There **happens to be** an extra seat.*

### There must be / there might be

- *There **must be** a reason.*
- *There **might be** a delay.*
- *There **can't be** that many.*

### Negation

- *There **isn't** any water.*
- *There **aren't** any problems.*
- *There **is no** excuse.*
- *There **was never** any doubt.*

### Existence vs identification

| Construction | Meaning |
|---|---|
| There is a problem | A problem exists |
| It is a problem | That thing = a problem |
| There is John | John exists/is present |
| It is John | That person = John |

### Existential there vs locative there

- *There **is** a book **there**.* (existential there + locative there)
- *There **are** my keys.* (pointing = locative, not existential)

### Inversion for emphasis

- *On the table **sat** three books.* (literary)
- *Among the guests **was** a famous writer.*

## Chinese

### <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby> (existence)

- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(There is a book.)
- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>多<rt>duō</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby>。(There are many people.)
- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>问<rt>wèn</rt></ruby><ruby>题<rt>tí</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？(Is there a problem?)

### Location + <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>

Chinese typically places the location first:

- <ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(On the table there is a book.)
- <ruby>房<rt>fáng</rt></ruby><ruby>间<rt>jiān</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>多<rt>duō</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby>。(In the room there are many people.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>咖<rt>kā</rt></ruby><ruby>啡<rt>fēi</rt></ruby>。(There is coffee here.)

### <ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby> (negation)

- <ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>水<rt>shuǐ</rt></ruby>。(There is no water.)
- <ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby>。(There is nobody.)
- <ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>问<rt>wèn</rt></ruby><ruby>题<rt>tí</rt></ruby>。(There is no problem.)

### <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby> (question)

- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby>？(Is there anyone?)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>里<rt>lǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>？(Are there books here?)

### <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> for change of state

- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Someone has come. — new existence)
- <ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>事<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(Something happened. — new event)

### <ruby>从<rt>cóng</rt></ruby><ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>... (once upon a time)

- <ruby>从<rt>cóng</rt></ruby><ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby>... (Once upon a time there was a person...)

### <ruby>存<rt>cún</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> (formal existence)

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>种<rt>zhǒng</rt></ruby><ruby>现<rt>xiàn</rt></ruby><ruby>象<rt>xiàng</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>存<rt>cún</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>。(This phenomenon does not exist. — formal)
- <ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby><ruby>命<rt>mìng</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>存<rt>cún</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>。(The existence of life.)

### Distinguishing <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>, <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>

| Construction | Meaning |
|---|---|
| <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> | There is a book (existence) |
| <ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby> | The book is on the table (location) |
| <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> | I have a book (possession) |
| <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> | This is a book (identification) |

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Is a book* → *There **is** a book* (dummy subject required)

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Es un libro* for existence → ***Hay** un libro* (use haber, not ser)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>**<ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>**<ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>* (location precedes <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *Have a book on the table* → *There **is** a book on the table*
