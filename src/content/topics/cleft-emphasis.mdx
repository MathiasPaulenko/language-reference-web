## Overview

Emphasis and cleft sentences allow speakers to highlight specific information: who did something, when it happened, how it was done, or to contrast with expectations.

- **Spanish**: Uses *es...que/quien* clefts. Word order is flexible, so fronting is common. Stress and particles (*sí*, *sólo*) add emphasis.
- **English**: Uses *it is...that/who* clefts and *what* pseudo-clefts. Stress (intonation) is crucial in spoken English. Negative fronting triggers inversion.
- **Chinese**: Uses *<ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>* for emphasis on time, manner, or agent. Topic-comment structure naturally front-shifts information. No verb change or inversion.

## Spanish

### Es...que/quien (cleft sentences)

Spanish clefts use *ser* + the emphasized element + *que/quien* + clause:

**Emphasizing a person**:

- *Fue **Juan** quien me llamó.* (It was Juan who called me.)
- *Fue **ella** la que ganó.* (It was she who won.)

**Emphasizing a thing**:

- *Fue **el dinero** lo que faltaba.* (It was money that was lacking.)
- *Fue **en Madrid** donde nos conocimos.* (It was in Madrid where we met.)

**Emphasizing time**:

- *Fue **ayer** cuando llegó.* (It was yesterday when he arrived.)
- *Fue **en 1992** cuando ocurrió.* (It was in 1992 when it happened.)

**Emphasizing manner**:

- *Fue **así** como lo hizo.* (It was like this that he did it.)

### Lo que... (pseudo-cleft)

- *Lo que quiero es **paz**.* (What I want is peace.)
- *Lo que me molesta es **el ruido**.* (What bothers me is the noise.)
- *Lo que necesitamos es **tiempo**.* (What we need is time.)

### Fronting (topicalization)

Spanish often moves elements to the front for emphasis:

- *Eso **no lo sé**.* (That I don't know. — object fronted)
- *A María **la vi** ayer.* (María I saw yesterday. — object fronted)
- *Rápido **lo hizo**.* (Quickly he did it. — adverb fronted)

### Emphatic particles

| Particle | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| sí | emphatic affirmation | *Sí que lo sé.* (I DO know it.) |
| solo / solamente | only | *Solo él lo sabe.* (Only he knows.) |
| ni siquiera | not even | *Ni siquiera lo intentó.* (He didn't even try.) |
| incluso | even | *Incluso ella lo sabe.* (Even she knows.) |

### Negative fronting with inversion

- *Nunca **he visto** tal cosa.* (Never have I seen such a thing.)
- *Rara vez **viene** por aquí.* (Rarely does he come here.)

**Note**: Inversion is less strict than in English and often optional.

## English

### It is...that/who (cleft sentences)

English clefts use *it is/was* + emphasized element + *that/who/which*:

**Emphasizing a person**:

- *It was **John** who left.*
- *It was **Mary** that I saw.* (that also acceptable for people in informal English)

**Emphasizing a thing**:

- *It was **the dog** that ate my homework.*
- *It was **in Paris** that we met.*

**Emphasizing time**:

- *It was **yesterday** that he left.*
- *It was **at 3 PM** when the phone rang.*

**Emphasizing reason**:

- *It was **because of you** that we failed.*

**Emphasizing manner**:

- *It was **by accident** that I found it.*

### What...is (pseudo-cleft)

- *What I want is **peace**.*
- *What surprised me was **his reaction**.*
- *What we need is **more time**.*

**All-cleft** (emphasizing everything):

- *All I want is **your love**.*
- *All she did was **complain**.*

### Reversed pseudo-cleft

- *Peace is **what I want**.*
- *His reaction is **what surprised me**.*

### Stress (intonation)

In spoken English, stress is the primary means of emphasis:

- *I **did** tell you.* (contrastive — you said I didn't)
- *I did **tell** you.* (contrastive — I didn't hide it)
- *I did tell **you**.* (contrastive — not someone else)

**In writing**, use italics, capitals, or adverbs:

- *I **did** tell you.* / *I DID tell you.* / *I certainly told you.*

### Negative fronting with inversion

When a negative adverb is fronted, the subject and auxiliary invert:

- *Never **have** I seen such beauty.*
- *Rarely **does** he complain.*
- *Not until yesterday **did** I understand.*
- *Only then **did** she realize.*
- *Hardly **had** I arrived when it started raining.*

**No inversion** without fronting:

- *I have **never** seen such beauty.* (normal order)

### Fronting without inversion

Objects and adverbs can be fronted for emphasis without inversion:

- *This book **I have read**.* (object fronted)
- *Quickly **he ran**.* (adverb fronted)
- *A fool **he is not**.* (predicate fronted)

## Chinese

### <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> — the core emphasis structure

Chinese uses <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> to emphasize **time, manner, place, or agent** of a completed action:

**Emphasizing time**:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was yesterday that I came.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>月<rt>yuè</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was last month that he left.)

**Emphasizing manner**:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>飞<rt>fēi</rt></ruby><ruby>机<rt>jī</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was by plane that I came.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby><ruby>朋<rt>péng</rt></ruby><ruby>友<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was with friends that he went.)

**Emphasizing agent**:

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>写<rt>xiě</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was I who wrote this book.)

**Emphasizing place**:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>men</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>认<rt>rèn</rt></ruby><ruby>识<rt>shi</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was in Beijing that we met.)

### When NOT to use <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>

<ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> is **only** for emphasis on background information. Do not use it for:

- Simple statements: ❌ <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (wrong — just say <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby>)
- Current actions: ❌ <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (wrong — say <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby>)
- Future actions: ❌ <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>明<rt>míng</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (wrong — say <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>明<rt>míng</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby>)

### Topic-comment (natural emphasis)

Chinese naturally emphasizes by putting the topic first:

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(This, I don't know.)
- <ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>，<ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>间<rt>jiān</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>。(Money I have; time I don't.)
- <ruby>中<rt>zhōng</rt></ruby><ruby>文<rt>wén</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>三<rt>sān</rt></ruby><ruby>年<rt>nián</rt></ruby>。(Chinese, I've studied for three years.)

### <ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby> and <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby> for emphasis

<ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby> = "only then" (later than expected):

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>八<rt>bā</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(He didn't come until eight. — emphasis on lateness)

<ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby> = "as early as" (earlier than expected):

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>七<rt>qī</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He came as early as seven. — emphasis on earliness)

### Contrastive emphasis with <ruby>确<rt>què</rt></ruby><ruby>实<rt>shí</rt></ruby> / <ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>确<rt>què</rt></ruby><ruby>实<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(I really don't know.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(I really did see it.)

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Overusing <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby>* (simple statement)

2. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Omitting inversion: *Not until he left I cried* → *Not until he left **did** I cry*

3. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using que for people: *Es Juan que vi* → *Es Juan **a quien** vi*

4. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Adding "do" unnecessarily: *I do love you* (romantic but not contrastive) vs *I DO love you* (you said I didn't)
