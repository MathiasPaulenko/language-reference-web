## Overview

Syntax is the study of how words combine into phrases, clauses, and sentences. Constituents are groups of words that function as a unit.

- **Spanish**: SVO basic order but with relatively free word order due to rich inflection. Constituent structure parallels English closely. Adjectives usually follow nouns.
- **English**: Rigid SVO order. Constituent structure is well-defined: NP, VP, PP, AdjP, AdvP. Movement rules (question formation, passivization) depend on constituent structure.
- **Chinese**: SVO basic order but with extensive topic-fronting. Constituents are marked by particles (<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>, <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>, <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>) rather than inflection. Pre-nominal relative clauses and modifiers are the norm.

## Spanish

### Basic constituent order

Spanish is SVO but with flexibility:

- *El estudiante lee un libro.* (SVO — neutral)
- *Un libro lee el estudiante.* (OSV — marked, literary)
- *Lee el estudiante un libro.* (VSO — marked)

**Note**: Word order changes information structure, not basic grammar.

### Noun phrase (NP)

**Determiner + Noun + Adjective**:

- *el libro interesante* (the interesting book)
- *una casa grande* (a big house)
- *mi amigo español* (my Spanish friend)

**Demonstrative + Noun**:

- *este libro* (this book)
- *aquella mujer* (that woman)

### Verb phrase (VP)

**Verb + Object + Adverb**:

- *lee rápidamente* (reads quickly)
- *escribe cartas a menudo* (writes letters often)

**Auxiliary + Main verb**:

- *ha leído* (has read)
- *está leyendo* (is reading)
- *puede venir* (can come)

### Prepositional phrase (PP)

**Preposition + NP**:

- *en la mesa* (on the table)
- *con Juan* (with Juan)
- *para María* (for María)

### Clause (CP/IP)

**Complementizer + IP**:

- *dijo **que** vendría* (said that he would come)
- *me pregunto **si** está* (I wonder if he is)

### Relative clause (CP)

Post-nominal:

- *el libro **que leí*** (the book that I read)
- *la mujer **a quien vi*** (the woman whom I saw)

### Adjective phrase (AdjP)

- *muy interesante* (very interesting)
- *bastante grande* (quite big)

### Adverbial phrase (AdvP)

- *muy rápidamente* (very quickly)
- *demasiado tarde* (too late)

## English

### Basic constituent order

English has rigid SVO order:

- *The student reads a book.* (SVO)
- **A book the student reads.** (marked, poetic)

Movement rules require do-support or auxiliary inversion:

- *Does the student read a book?* (auxiliary inversion)
- *What does the student read?* (wh-movement)
- *A book is read by the student.* (passive)

### Noun phrase (NP)

**Determiner + Adjective + Noun**:

- *the interesting book*
- *a big house*
- *my Spanish friend*

**Demonstrative + Adjective + Noun**:

- *this interesting book*
- *that big house*

### Verb phrase (VP)

**Auxiliary chain + Main verb**:

- *has been reading* (VP: has [been [reading]])
- *can have been eaten* (modal + perfect + passive)

**Verb + Object + PP**:

- *put the book on the table*
- *gave a gift to Mary*

### Prepositional phrase (PP)

- *on the table*
- *with John*
- *for Mary*
- *in the morning*

**Particle vs preposition**:

- *turn **on** the light* (particle — separable)
- *turn **on** the table* (preposition — not separable)

### Clause (CP/IP)

**Complementizer + Subject + Auxiliary + Verb**:

- *said that he **would** come*
- *wondered if she **had** left*
- *asked whether they **were** ready*

### Relative clause (CP)

Post-nominal, with gap:

- *the book **that I read ___***
- *the woman **whom I saw ___***
- *the reason **why I left ___***

### Adjective phrase (AdjP)

- *very interesting*
- *quite big*
- *too difficult*
- *old enough*

### Adverbial phrase (AdvP)

- *very quickly*
- *too carefully*
- *quite well*

### Coordination

- *[NP the student] and [NP the teacher]*
- *[VP reads] and [VP writes]*
- *[S he came] but [S she left]*

## Chinese

### Topic-comment structure

Chinese frequently uses topic-comment rather than strict SVO:

- <ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(As for the book, I have finished reading it.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>件<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby><ruby>事<rt>shì</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(As for this matter, I don't know.)

**Note**: The topic is not necessarily the grammatical subject.

### Noun phrase (NP)

**No articles, optional classifier**:

- <ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby> (student / the student / a student)
- <ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby> (a student — with classifier)
- <ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby> (that student)

**Adjective + <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> + Noun**:

- <ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby> (the tall student)
- <ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>趣<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (the interesting book)

### Verb phrase (VP)

**Verb + Resultative complement**:

- <ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> (read-finish = finish reading)
- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby> (look-perceive = see)
- <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby><ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby> (listen-understand = understand by hearing)

**Verb + Directional complement**:

- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (walk-up-here)
- <ruby>拿<rt>ná</rt></ruby><ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (take-out-there)

**Aspect markers**:

- <ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (read-LE = completed)
- <ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> (read-GUO = experienced)
- <ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> (read-ZHE = ongoing)

### Prepositional phrase (PP)

**Coverb + NP** (<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>从<rt>cóng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby>, <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby>, <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby>):

- <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby> (on the table)
- <ruby>从<rt>cóng</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby> (from Beijing)
- <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>校<rt>xiào</rt></ruby> (to school)
- <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>玛<rt>Mǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>丽<rt>lì</rt></ruby> (for María)

### Relative clause (CP)

Pre-nominal, marked with <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (the book that I read)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>车<rt>chē</rt></ruby> (the car that he bought)

### Adjective phrase (AdjP)

**Adverb + <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> + Adjective**:

- <ruby>非<rt>fēi</rt></ruby><ruby>常<rt>cháng</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (very good)
- <ruby>太<rt>tài</rt></ruby><ruby>大<rt>dà</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (too big)

### Adverbial phrase (AdvP)

**Adjective + <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> + Verb**:

- <ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>速<rt>sù</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby> (quickly read)
- <ruby>认<rt>rèn</rt></ruby><ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>写<rt>xiě</rt></ruby> (carefully write)

**Note**: <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> marks adverbial modification of a verb (distinct from <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> and <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>).

### Resultative complement (<ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>)

**Verb + <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> + Degree + Adjective**:

- <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby> (runs very fast)
- <ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (speaks very well)

**Note**: <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> marks the degree/result of a verb (distinct from <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> and <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>).

### Three DEs (<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>, <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>, <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>)

| Character | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> | attributive (modifier + noun) | <ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>生<rt>shēng</rt></ruby> (tall student) |
| <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> | adverbial (adverb + verb) | <ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>速<rt>sù</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby> (quickly read) |
| <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> | resultative (verb + degree) | <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby> (run very fast) |

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Basic order | SVO (flexible) | SVO (rigid) | SVO (topic-comment common) |
| NP structure | Det + N + Adj | Det + Adj + N | (Num + CL) + Adj + <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> + N |
| VP structure | Aux + Main verb | Aux chain + Main verb | Verb + complement + aspect |
| PP structure | Preposition + NP | Preposition + NP | Coverb + NP |
| Relative clause | Post-nominal | Post-nominal | Pre-nominal (<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>) |
| Adverb marking | -mente suffix | -ly suffix | <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> particle |
| Result marking | Reflexive / periphrasis | Get / become + Adj | <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> particle |
| Topic fronting | Literary | Marked | Extremely common |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *el interesante libro* → *el libro **interesante*** (post-nominal adjective)

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *the book interesting* → *the **interesting** book* (pre-nominal adjective)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>速<rt>sù</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>速<rt>sù</rt></ruby>**<ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>**<ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>* (adverb requires <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *He runs very fastly* → *He runs very **fast*** (fast is both adj and adv)
