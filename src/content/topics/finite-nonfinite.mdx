## Overview

Finite verbs carry tense, mood, and agreement with a subject. Non-finite verbs (infinitives, participles, gerunds) do not.

- **Spanish**: Clear distinction. Finite verbs conjugate for person, number, tense, and mood. Non-finite forms are the infinitive, gerund (*-ndo*), and past participle. Infinitives often replace subjunctive clauses when subjects are shared.
- **English**: Clear distinction. Finite verbs agree in the 3rd person singular present. Non-finite forms are the to-infinitive, bare infinitive, present participle/gerund (*-ing*), and past participle (*-ed/-en*).
- **Chinese**: No formal distinction. Verbs are invariant. Aspect markers (*<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>, <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby>, <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby>*) apply to verbs regardless of their syntactic position. The concept of finiteness is not grammatically relevant.

## Spanish

### Finite verb forms

Finite verbs carry person, number, tense, and mood:

- *Yo **hablo**.* (I speak. — 1st person, singular, present, indicative)
- *Él **vendrá**.* (He will come. — 3rd person, singular, future, indicative)
- *Si **tuviera** tiempo...* (If I had time... — subjunctive)
- ***Ven** aquí.* (Come here. — imperative)

### Non-finite forms

| Form | Ending | Example | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infinitive | -ar/-er/-ir | hablar, comer, vivir | noun, complement |
| Gerund | -ando/-iendo | hablando, comiendo | progressive, adverbial |
| Past participle | -ado/-ido | hablado, comido | compound tenses, passive |

### Infinitive as subject

- ***Hablar** es fácil.* (Speaking is easy.)
- ***Mentir** no está bien.* (Lying is not right.)

### Infinitive as complement (same subject)

- *Quiero **ir**.* (I want to go.)
- *Puedo **ayudarte**.* (I can help you.)
- *Espero **llegar** pronto.* (I hope to arrive soon.)

**Note**: When subjects differ, Spanish uses subjunctive:

- *Quiero **que vengas**.* (I want you to come. — different subject)

### Infinitive after prepositions

- *antes de **comer*** (before eating)
- *sin **decir** nada* (without saying anything)
- *para **estudiar*** (in order to study)
- *al **llegar*** (upon arriving)

### Gerund (-ndo)

**Progressive**:
- *Estoy **leyendo**.* (I am reading.)
- *Estaba **durmiendo**.* (I was sleeping.)

**Adverbial** (simultaneity, manner, cause):
- ***Estando** en Madrid, la vi.* (Being in Madrid, I saw her.)
- ***Corriendo**, llegó a tiempo.* (Running, he arrived on time.)

### Past participle

**Compound tenses**:
- *He **hablado**.* (I have spoken.)
- *Había **comido**.* (I had eaten.)

**Passive**:
- *Fue **visto** por todos.* (He was seen by everyone.)

**Adjectival**:
- *la puerta **cerrada*** (the closed door)
- *un hombre **cansado*** (a tired man)

### Non-finite forms with auxiliary verbs

- *Tengo que **ir**.* (I have to go.)
- *Voy a **comer**.* (I'm going to eat.)
- *Acabo de **llegar**.* (I just arrived.)
- *Estoy por **salir**.* (I'm about to leave.)

## English

### Finite verb forms

Finite verbs agree in the 3rd person singular present:

- *I **speak**.* / *He **speaks**.*
- *She **went**.* / *They **went**.*
- *If I **were** rich...* (subjunctive)
- ***Come** here!* (imperative — finite but unmarked)

### Non-finite forms

| Form | Example | Use |
|---|---|---|
| To-infinitive | to speak, to eat | complement, purpose |
| Bare infinitive | speak, eat | after modals, causatives, perception |
| Present participle/gerund | speaking, eating | progressive, gerund, modifier |
| Past participle | spoken, eaten | perfect, passive, modifier |

### To-infinitive

**As subject**:
- ***To err** is human.*
- ***To speak** clearly is important.*

**As complement**:
- *I want **to go**.*
- *She hopes **to win**.*
- *He decided **to stay**.*

**For purpose**:
- *I came **to help**.*
- *This tool is used **to cut** wood.*

**After adjectives**:
- *I am happy **to see** you.*
- *It is important **to study**.*

### Bare infinitive

**After modals**:
- *I **can go**.* / *She **must leave**.*

**After causatives**:
- *I made him **go**.* (NOT *to go*)
- *Let me **help**.*
- *Have them **call** me.*

**After perception verbs**:
- *I saw him **leave**.* (NOT *to leave*)
- *I heard her **sing**.*

### Present participle / gerund (-ing)

**Progressive (finite auxiliary + non-finite participle)**:
- *I am **reading**.*
- *She was **sleeping**.*

**Gerund (noun use)**:
- ***Reading** is fun.*
- *I enjoy **swimming**.*

**Adjectival modifier**:
- *the **running** water*
- *a **sleeping** child*

**Adverbial**:
- ***Walking** down the street, I saw her.*

### Past participle (-ed/-en)

**Perfect (finite auxiliary + non-finite participle)**:
- *I have **spoken**.*
- *She had **eaten**.*

**Passive (finite auxiliary + non-finite participle)**:
- *The letter was **written**.*
- *He was **seen** by everyone.*

**Adjectival modifier**:
- *a **broken** heart*
- *the **spoken** word*

### Verbal noun vs gerund

- ***Swimming** is healthy.* (gerund — no article)
- *The **swimming** of dolphins is graceful.* (verbal noun — with article, of-phrase)

## Chinese

### No finite/non-finite distinction

Chinese verbs do not change form whether they are main verbs, complements, or modifiers:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>。(I leave. — finite-like)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>。(I want to leave. — same form)
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>。(Leaving is good. — same form as subject)

### Verbs as subjects (no special form)

- <ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>习<rt>xí</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>重<rt>zhòng</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby>。(Studying is important.)
- <ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>谎<rt>huǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby>。(Lying is wrong.)

### Verbs as complements (no special form)

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>。(I want to leave.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>会<rt>huì</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(He can come.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huan</rt></ruby><ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby>。(I like swimming.)

### <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> for nominalization

Chinese uses <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> to nominalize a verb phrase:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>事<rt>shì</rt></ruby> (the things I do — nominalized clause)
- <ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>人<rt>rén</rt></ruby> (people who read — relative)
- <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>动<rt>dòng</rt></ruby><ruby>作<rt>zuò</rt></ruby> (the action of running)

### Aspect markers (not finiteness markers)

Chinese aspect markers apply regardless of syntactic position:

- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (left — perfective)
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> (walking — durative)
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> (have walked — experiential)

**Note**: <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>, <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby>, <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> are not finiteness markers. They indicate aspect and can appear on verbs in any syntactic position.

### Coverbs as prepositions

Chinese coverbs function like prepositions but derive from verbs:

- <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>习<rt>xí</rt></ruby> (study in Beijing — <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> = at/in)
- <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby><ruby>笔<rt>bǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>写<rt>xiě</rt></ruby> (write with a pen — <ruby>用<rt>yòng</rt></ruby> = use/with)
- <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>玛<rt>Mǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>丽<rt>lì</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (buy a book for María — <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby> = give/for)

**Note**: Coverbs are a distinct category in Chinese, bridging verbs and prepositions.

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Infinitive | hablar | to speak / speak | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (same form) |
| Gerund/participle | hablando | speaking | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (same form) |
| Past participle | hablado | spoken | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (same form) |
| Progressive | estar + -ndo | be + -ing | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> + verb |
| Perfect | haber + -ado/-ido | have + -ed/-en | verb + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>/<ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> |
| Passive | ser + -ado/-ido | be + -ed/-en | <ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby> + verb |
| Same-subject complement | infinitive | to-infinitive | bare verb |
| Different-subject complement | subjunctive | for/to + clause | clause/clause |
| Finiteness distinction | Yes | Yes | No |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Me gusta nadando* → *Me gusta **nadar*** (infinitive, not gerund)

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *I want that I go* → *I want **to go*** (infinitive with shared subject)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby>to<ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby>**<ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>*** (no infinitive marker)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *I made him to go* → *I made him **go*** (bare infinitive)
