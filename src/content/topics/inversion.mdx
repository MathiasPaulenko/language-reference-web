## Overview

Inversion reverses the normal subject-verb order. Fronting moves an element to the beginning of the sentence for emphasis.

- **Spanish**: Inverts freely in questions without auxiliaries. Fronts elements for focus but rarely uses true subject-verb inversion for emphasis. Clefts use *ser + quien/que*.
- **English**: Inverts only after certain fronted negative adverbs (*never, rarely, hardly, scarcely, little, only then*). Questions require auxiliary inversion. Clefts use *it is/was...who/that*.
- **Chinese**: Does not invert at all. Uses the *<ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>* cleft construction for emphasis, and topic-fronting for information structure.

## Spanish

### Question inversion

Spanish inverts subject and verb directly, without auxiliaries:

- *¿**Viene** Juan?* (Is Juan coming?)
- *¿**Está** aquí María?* (Is María here?)
- *¿**Tiene** hambre?* (Is he hungry?)

**With wh-words**:

- *¿**Dónde** está Juan?* (Where is Juan?)
- *¿**Qué** quieres?* (What do you want?)
- *¿**Cuándo** llega?* (When does he arrive?)

### Fronting (no inversion)

Spanish moves elements to the front without changing subject-verb order:

- ***A Juan** le di el libro.* (To Juan I gave the book.)
- ***El libro** se lo di a Juan.* (The book I gave to Juan.)
- ***En la mesa** lo dejé.* (On the table I left it.)

### Cleft sentences (ser + quien/que)

- *Fue **Juan quien** lo rompió.* (It was Juan who broke it.)
- *Fue **en Madrid donde** nos conocimos.* (It was in Madrid where we met.)
- *Fue **ayer cuando** llegó.* (It was yesterday when he arrived.)

### Locative inversion (limited)

- *Aquí **está** tu libro.* (Here is your book.)
- *Ahí **viene** el bus.* (There comes the bus.)

### After nunca / rara vez

No inversion; fronting only:

- *Nunca **he visto** tal cosa.* (Never have I seen such a thing. — Spanish: no inversion)
- *Rara vez **se queja**.* (Rarely does he complain. — Spanish: no inversion)

### Conditional clauses

No inversion in conditionals:

- *Si **hubiera** sabido...* (If I had known...)
- *Si **fuera** rico...* (If I were rich...)

## English

### Question inversion

Yes/No questions invert subject and auxiliary:

- ***Is** Juan **coming**?*
- ***Does** he **speak** English?*
- ***Have** you **seen** him?*

Wh-questions invert if the wh-word is not the subject:

- ***Where is** Juan?* (Juan = subject complement)
- *Who **is coming**?* (who = subject — no inversion)
- ***What did** you **say**?*

### Inversion after negative adverbs

When a negative or limiting adverb is fronted, the subject and auxiliary invert:

- ***Never have** I **seen** such beauty.*
- ***Rarely does** he **complain**.*
- ***Hardly had** I **arrived** when the phone rang.*
- ***Little did** they **know** what awaited them.*
- ***Scarcely had** she **finished** when he arrived.*
- ***Not only did** he **win**, but he also broke the record.*
- ***No sooner had** I **arrived** than he left.*

### Only then / Only when / Only after

- ***Only then did** I **understand**.*
- ***Only when** it was too late **did** she **realize**.*
- ***Only after** he left **did** I **cry**.*

### Here / There + verb + subject

Locative inversion (mainly with verbs of motion and existence):

- *Here **comes** the bus.*
- *There **goes** my chance.*
- *Here **is** your book.*
- *There **lies** the problem.*

**Note**: The subject must be definite:

- ✅ *Here **comes** the bus.*
- ❌ *Here **comes** a bus.* (marginal)

### Conditional inversion

Omitting *if* and inverting:

- ***Had** I **known**, I would have come.* (= If I had known...)
- ***Were** I **rich**, I would buy a house.* (= If I were rich...)
- ***Should** you **see** him, tell him to call me.* (= If you should see him...)

**Note**: Only for hypothetical/subjunctive conditionals.

### Cleft sentences (it-cleft)

- *It was **John who** broke it.*
- *It was **in Madrid that** we met.*
- *It was **yesterday that** he arrived.*

### Pseudo-clefts

- ***What** I want **is** peace.*
- ***What** surprised me **was** his reaction.*

## Chinese

### No inversion for questions

Chinese never inverts for questions:

- <ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？(Is Juan coming? — <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> marks the question)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>哪<rt>nǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>儿<rt>er</rt></ruby>？(Where is he? — normal order)

### <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> cleft (emphasis)

The most common emphatic construction in Chinese:

- <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was John who broke it.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was yesterday that I came.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>认<rt>rèn</rt></ruby><ruby>识<rt>shi</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was in Beijing that I met him.)

**Pattern**: <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> + emphasized element + verb + <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>

### Topic fronting (no inversion)

Chinese freely moves topics to the front:

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>已<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>经<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(This book, I have already read.)
- <ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>三<rt>sān</rt></ruby><ruby>次<rt>cì</rt></ruby>。(Beijing, I have been three times.)

### <ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby> fronting (object preposing)

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>放<rt>fàng</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(I put the book on the table. — book is fronted)

### <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby>...<ruby>都<rt>dū</rt></ruby>/<ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby> (even)

Emphatic fronting with <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby>:

- <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(Even he doesn't know.)
- <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby><ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>会<rt>huì</rt></ruby>。(Even this I can't do.)

### <ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby> and <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby> (delayed/immediate realization)

- <ruby>那<rt>nà</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hou</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby><ruby>明<rt>míng</rt></ruby><ruby>白<rt>bái</rt></ruby>。(Only then did I understand. — <ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby> marks delayed realization)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>少<rt>shǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>抱<rt>bào</rt></ruby><ruby>怨<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby>。(Rarely do I complain. — <ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>少<rt>shǎo</rt></ruby> = rarely)

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Never I have seen* → *Never **have I** seen*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Solo entonces did I entender* → *Solo entonces **entendí*** (no inversion)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>**<ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>*** (no inversion)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *Rarely he complains* → *Rarely **does he** complain*
