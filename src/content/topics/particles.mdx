## Overview

Particles are small function words that express grammatical features. Chinese is famous for its particle system, but every language has them.

- **Spanish**: Encodes aspect, tense, and mood through verb conjugation. Particles are minimal — mainly conjunctions and discourse markers.
- **English**: Uses auxiliary verbs (have, be, will, do) plus word order and intonation. Particles exist (to, up, out in phrasal verbs) but are different from Chinese.
- **Chinese**: Relies on a rich system of particles that follow verbs, nouns, or entire sentences to express aspect, mood, possession, and degree.

## Spanish

### Aspect and tense on the verb

Spanish encodes most grammatical information through verb endings:

- *habl**é*** (I spoke — preterite, completed)
- *habl**aba*** (I was speaking — imperfect, ongoing)
- *he habl**ado*** (I have spoken — perfect, experience)
- *estoy habl**ando*** (I am speaking — progressive)

### Discourse particles

Spanish has some sentence-final and interjective particles:

| Particle | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ¿...? | Question brackets | *¿Vienes?* |
| pues | well, so | *Pues, no sé.* |
| o sea | that is, I mean | *O sea, no me gusta.* |
| vamos | come on | *¡Vamos, apúrate!* |
| hombre / mujer | hey (interjection) | *Hombre, ¡qué sorpresa!* |
| ¿verdad? / ¿no? | tag question | *Es tarde, ¿verdad?* |
| eh | hesitation | *Voy a, eh, comprar pan.* |

### Prepositional particles

Prepositions act like particles when combined with verbs:

- *acabar **de*** + infinitive (have just)
- *volver **a*** + infinitive (do again)
- *dejar **de*** + infinitive (stop doing)

## English

### Auxiliary particles

English uses auxiliaries where Chinese uses particles:

| Function | Auxiliary | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Past completed | did | *I **did** see him.* |
| Progressive | be + -ing | *I **am** going.* |
| Perfect | have + -ed | *I **have** finished.* |
| Passive | be + -ed | *I **was** seen.* |
| Future | will / going to | *I **will** call.* |
| Negation | do + not | *I **do** not know.* |
| Question | do / be / have + subject | ***Do** you know?* |

### Phrasal verb particles

English uses particles with verbs to create idiomatic meanings:

| Particle | Directional | Idiomatic |
|---|---|---|
| up | climb up | give up (surrender) |
| down | sit down | calm down |
| off | fall off | take off (leave) |
| on | put on | carry on (continue) |
| out | go out | figure out (understand) |
| away | run away | put away (store) |
| back | come back | take back (retract) |

### Discourse particles

| Particle | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| well | hesitation, concession | *Well, I'm not sure.* |
| so | transition | *So, what happened?* |
| you know | hedging, seeking agreement | *It's, you know, difficult.* |
| I mean | clarification | *I mean, it's fine.* |
| like | quotative, approximation | *He was like, "No way."* |
| right? / okay? | tag question | *It's cold, right?* |
| huh? | confirmation | *You're coming, huh?* |

## Chinese

Chinese particles are the backbone of its grammar. They express what other languages encode through conjugation or auxiliaries.

### Aspect particles

| Particle | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> | completion / change | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(I ate. / I've eaten.) |
| <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> | past experience | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>。(I've been to Beijing.) |
| <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> | ongoing action | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby>。(I'm eating.) |
| <ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> | continuous state | <ruby>门<rt>mén</rt></ruby><ruby>开<rt>kāi</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby>。(The door is open.) |

**<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>** nuances:

- Verb + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> = completed action: <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (ate)
- <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> at end of sentence = change of state: <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (It's raining now. — change)
- Both: <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (I've finished eating.)

### Sentence-final mood particles

| Particle | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> | yes/no question | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？(How are you?) |
| <ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby> | follow-up question | <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>？(And you?) |
| <ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby> | suggestion, softening | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>吧<rt>ba</rt></ruby>！(Let's go!) |
| <ruby>啊<rt>a</rt></ruby> | exclamation, emotion | <ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>啊<rt>a</rt></ruby>！(Great!) |
| <ruby>嘛<rt>ma</rt></ruby> | obviousness, persuasion | <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>嘛<rt>ma</rt></ruby>！(Come on!) |
| <ruby>呗<rt>bei</rt></ruby> | resignation, obviousness | <ruby>随<rt>suí</rt></ruby><ruby>便<rt>biàn</rt></ruby><ruby>呗<rt>bei</rt></ruby>。(Whatever.) |
| <ruby>呀<rt>ya</rt></ruby> | softening (variant of <ruby>啊<rt>ā</rt></ruby>) | <ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>呀<rt>ya</rt></ruby>！(Hurry and come!) |

### Structural particles: <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>, <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>, <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>

| Character | Pronunciation | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> | de | possession, modification | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (my book) |
| <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> | de | manner adverb marker | <ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (walk slowly) |
| <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> | de | degree complement marker | <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>得<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>快<rt>kuài</rt></ruby> (run very fast) |

### <ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby> construction

<ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby> is a particle that marks the direct object and raises it to pre-verbal position:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>放<rt>fàng</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>桌<rt>zhuō</rt></ruby><ruby>子<rt>zi</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>。(I put the book on the table.)
- <ruby>请<rt>qǐng</rt></ruby><ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>门<rt>mén</rt></ruby><ruby>关<rt>guān</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>。(Please close the door.)

**Rule**: <ruby>把<rt>bǎ</rt></ruby> requires a specific, definite object and a resulting state.

### <ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby> construction

<ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby> marks the passive:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby><ruby>批<rt>pī</rt></ruby><ruby>评<rt>píng</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He was criticized by the teacher.)

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Completion | Verb ending (-é) | Past simple (-ed) | <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> |
| Past experience | Perfect (he hablado) | Present perfect (have spoken) | <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> |
| Ongoing action | Progressive (estoy hablando) | Continuous (am speaking) | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> / <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> |
| Yes/no question | ¿...? + intonation | Auxiliary inversion / intonation | <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> |
| Suggestion | Vamos a / ¿Por qué no? | Let's / Why don't we | <ruby>吧<rt>bā</rt></ruby> |
| Possession | de / adjective | 's / of | <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> |
| Degree | muy + adjective | very + adjective | <ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby> + complement |
| Manner adverb | adjective + -mente | adjective + -ly | adjective + <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> |
| Passive | ser + participle | be + participle | <ruby>被<rt>bèi</rt></ruby> |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Adding <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> to every past event: *<ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>商<rt>shāng</rt></ruby><ruby>店<rt>diàn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>商<rt>shāng</rt></ruby><ruby>店<rt>diàn</rt></ruby>* (simple past narrative, no <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> needed)

2. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Using <ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby> with <ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>吗<rt>ma</rt></ruby>？* → *<ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>为<rt>wéi</rt></ruby><ruby>什<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>么<rt>mó</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>？*

3. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Omitting auxiliaries: *I go now* → *I **am** going now*

4. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Confusing <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>/<ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby>/<ruby>得<rt>dé</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>慢<rt>màn</rt></ruby><ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>* (needs <ruby>地<rt>dì</rt></ruby> for adverb)
