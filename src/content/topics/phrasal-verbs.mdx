## Overview

Phrasal verbs are a distinctive feature of English where a verb combines with a particle to create a new meaning.

- **English**: Thousands of phrasal verbs. They are separable (verb + object + particle) or inseparable (verb + particle + object). Often the most natural way to express an action in informal English.
- **Spanish**: No phrasal verb system. Uses simple verbs (*buscar* = look for), reflexives (*rendirse* = give up), or periphrastic constructions.
- **Chinese**: No phrasal verbs. Uses compound verbs (<ruby>放<rt>fàng</rt></ruby><ruby>弃<rt>qì</rt></ruby>), directional complements (<ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>), or resultative compounds (<ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby>).

## Spanish

### No phrasal verbs

Spanish does not combine verbs with particles like English. Instead it uses:

**Simple verbs**:
- *buscar* (look for)
- *encontrar* (find / come across)
- *cuidar* (look after)
- *soportar* (put up with)
- *devolver* (give back)
- *continuar* (carry on)

**Reflexive verbs**:
- *rendirse* (give up — surrender-reflexive)
- *estropearse* (break down — break-reflexive)
- *despertarse* (wake up)
- *levantarse* (get up)

**Periphrastic constructions**:
- *quedarse sin* (run out of)
- *echar de menos* (miss someone)
- *hacer caso de* (pay attention to)

### Common equivalents

| English phrasal | Spanish equivalent |
|---|---|
| look for | buscar |
| look after | cuidar |
| give up | rendirse |
| put off | posponer |
| turn on | encender |
| turn off | apagar |
| get up | levantarse |
| wake up | despertarse |
| come back | volver |
| go away | irse |
| sit down | sentarse |
| stand up | ponerse de pie |

### Particle-like elements

Spanish uses prepositions with verbs, but these are not phrasal verbs:

- *pensar **en*** (think about/of)
- *soñar **con*** (dream of)
- *depender **de*** (depend on)
- *confiar **en*** (trust in)

**Note**: These are verb + preposition combinations with predictable meaning, not idiomatic phrasal verbs.

## English

### Separable phrasal verbs

The object can go between the verb and the particle:

- *turn **the light** on* / *turn on the light*
- *put **your coat** away* / *put away your coat*
- *take **it** off* / ✅ *take off your shoes* (but NOT *take off it*)

**Pronouns must separate**:

- ✅ *turn **it** on*
- ❌ *turn on it*

### Inseparable phrasal verbs

The object cannot go between the verb and the particle:

- *look **after** the children* (NOT *look the children after*)
- *run **into** an old friend* (NOT *run an old friend into*)
- *get **along with** him* (NOT *get him along with*)
- *come **across** a book* (NOT *come a book across*)

### Three-part phrasal verbs

Verb + particle + preposition:

- *put **up with*** (tolerate)
- *look **forward to*** (anticipate)
- *get **on with*** (have a good relationship)
- *catch **up with*** (reach the same level)
- *run **out of*** (deplete)
- *look **down on*** (disrespect)
- *face **up to*** (confront)

**Note**: Three-part phrasal verbs are always inseparable.

### Literal vs idiomatic

| Phrasal verb | Literal | Idiomatic |
|---|---|---|
| take off | remove clothing | depart (airplane) |
| break down | break into pieces | stop functioning; analyze |
| give up | hand upward | surrender |
| look up | gaze upward | search for information |
| run over | drive over | review; overflow |

### Formality

Phrasal verbs are often more informal than Latinate equivalents:

| Phrasal (informal) | Latinate (formal) |
|---|---|
| give up | relinquish |
| put off | postpone |
| look into | investigate |
| come across | encounter |
| make up | invent / fabricate |
| turn down | reject |

## Chinese

### No phrasal verbs

Chinese does not have a phrasal verb system. It uses:

**Compound verbs**:

- <ruby>放<rt>fàng</rt></ruby><ruby>弃<rt>qì</rt></ruby> (give up — release-abandon)
- <ruby>照<rt>zhào</rt></ruby><ruby>顾<rt>gù</rt></ruby> (look after — shine-consider)
- <ruby>忍<rt>rěn</rt></ruby><ruby>受<rt>shòu</rt></ruby> (put up with — endure-receive)
- <ruby>关<rt>guān</rt></ruby><ruby>心<rt>xīn</rt></ruby> (care about — close-heart)
- <ruby>注<rt>zhù</rt></ruby><ruby>意<rt>yì</rt></ruby> (pay attention — pour-mind)

**Directional complements**:

Chinese adds directional words after the verb:

| Direction | Complement | Example |
|---|---|---|
| up | <ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (walk up here) |
| down | <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (walk down there) |
| in | <ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (walk in here) |
| out | <ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (walk out there) |
| back | <ruby>回<rt>huí</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>回<rt>huí</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> | <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>回<rt>huí</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (walk back here) |

**Resultative complements**:

- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby> (look-perceive = see)
- <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby><ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby> (listen-understand = understand by hearing)
- <ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> (do-finish = finish doing)
- <ruby>找<rt>zhǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby> (look for-reach = find)

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Turn on it* → *Turn **it on***

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Dar arriba* → **rendirse** (not literal)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>上<rt>shàng</rt></ruby>* for "give up" → <ruby>放<rt>fàng</rt></ruby><ruby>弃<rt>qì</rt></ruby> (compound verb)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *I looked the word up in the dictionary* → correct! But *I looked up it* → *I looked **it up***
