## Overview

Possession expresses who owns or is related to something.

- **Spanish**: Uses the preposition *de* for most possession. Possessive adjectives (*mi, tu, su*) agree in number with the possessed object. Body parts usually take a definite article, not a possessive.
- **English**: Uses the apostrophe-s ('s) for people and animals, or possessive pronouns (my, your, his, her, its, our, their). Possessive pronouns (mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs) stand alone.
- **Chinese**: Uses <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> after the owner. With close relationships (family, close friends), <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> is often omitted. No possessive pronouns distinct from possessive adjectives.

## Spanish

### De + noun (basic possession)

The most common way to show possession:

- *el coche **de** María* (Mary's car)
- *la casa **de** mis padres* (my parents' house)
- *un amigo **de** Juan* (a friend of Juan's)

**Never** use apostrophe-s in Spanish.

### Possessive adjectives

| Person | Singular possessed | Plural possessed |
|---|---|---|
| my | mi | mis |
| your (tú) | tu | tus |
| his/her/your (usted) | su | sus |
| our | nuestro/a | nuestros/as |
| your (vosotros) | vuestro/a | vuestros/as |
| their/your (ustedes) | su | sus |

**Agreement**: Possessives agree with the **possessed** noun, not the owner:

- *mi libro* (my book) / *mis libros* (my books)
- *nuestra casa* (our house) / *nuestros coches* (our cars)

**Note**: *su* is ambiguous — it can mean his, her, your (formal), or their.

### Possessive pronouns

| Person | Masculine singular | Feminine singular | Masculine plural | Feminine plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| mine | el mío | la mía | los míos | las mías |
| yours | el tuyo | la tuya | los tuyos | las tuyas |
| his/hers/yours | el suyo | la suya | los suyos | las suyas |
| ours | el nuestro | la nuestra | los nuestros | las nuestras |
| yours (pl) | el vuestro | la vuestra | los vuestros | las vuestras |
| theirs | el suyo | la suya | los suyos | las suyas |

- *Este libro es **mío**.* (This book is mine.)
- *Esa casa es **nuestra**.* (That house is ours.)

### Body parts — definite article, not possessive

With body parts, Spanish uses a definite article, with the possessor indicated by the verb or pronoun:

- *Me duele **la** cabeza.* (My head hurts. — lit. "The head hurts to me.")
- *Se lavó **las** manos.* (He washed his hands. — lit. "He washed the hands.")
- *Levanta **la** mano.* (Raise your hand. — lit. "Raise the hand.")

## English

### Apostrophe-s ('s)

Used for people, animals, and sometimes organizations:

- *John**'s** book* (singular owner)
- *the children**'s** toys* (plural ending in -n)
- *my parents**'** house* (plural ending in -s)
- *a week**'s** pay* (time expressions)

### Possessive adjectives

| Person | Adjective |
|---|---|
| I | my |
| you | your |
| he | his |
| she | her |
| it | its |
| we | our |
| they | their |

These always precede a noun:

- *This is **my** book.*
- *That is **her** car.*

### Possessive pronouns

| Person | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| I | mine |
| you | yours |
| he | his |
| she | hers |
| it | its (rare) |
| we | ours |
| they | theirs |

These stand alone, replacing noun + possessive adjective:

- *This book is **mine**.* (= This is my book.)
- *That car is **hers**.* (= That is her car.)

### Of-construction

For inanimate objects and longer phrases, English prefers *of*:

- *the roof **of** the house* (NOT the house's roof — though both are heard)
- *the name **of** the movie*
- *a friend **of** mine* (double possession)

### Its vs it's

- *its* = possessive: *The dog wagged **its** tail.*
- *it's* = it is / it has: ***It's** raining.* / ***It's** been a long day.*

## Chinese

### <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> as possession marker

<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> follows the owner:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (my book)
- <ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>车<rt>chē</rt></ruby> (John's car)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>们<rt>men</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>家<rt>jiā</rt></ruby> (our home)

### Omitting <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> with close relationships

With family and very close friends, <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> is often dropped:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>ba</rt></ruby> (my dad) — NOT <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>ba</rt></ruby> (sounds distant)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>妈<rt>mā</rt></ruby><ruby>妈<rt>ma</rt></ruby> (his mom)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>朋<rt>péng</rt></ruby><ruby>友<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby> (my friend) — <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> optional

**Rule**: Monosyllabic pronoun + close relationship noun → <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> usually omitted.

### No possessive pronouns

Chinese does not distinguish possessive adjectives from pronouns:

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(This is my book.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(This book is mine.)

Both use <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>.

### Multiple possession

Chinese chains <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>朋<rt>péng</rt></ruby><ruby>友<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>车<rt>chē</rt></ruby> (my friend's car)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>老<rt>lǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>师<rt>shī</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (his teacher's book)

### Body parts

Chinese often uses possessives for body parts, unlike Spanish:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>头<rt>tóu</rt></ruby><ruby>疼<rt>téng</rt></ruby>。(My head hurts.)
- <ruby>洗<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>手<rt>shǒu</rt></ruby>。(Wash hands.)

But the possessor can be omitted when context is clear.

## Comparison at a glance

| Feature | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|

| Possessive adjective | mi/mis, tu/tus... | my, your, his... | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>, <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>... |
| Agreement | Yes (with possessed) | No | No |
| Possessive pronoun | el mío/la mía... | mine, yours, his... | <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>, <ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>... (same as adjective) |
| Body parts | Definite article | Possessive adjective | Possessive or bare noun |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Apostrophe-s: *Maria's coche* → *el coche **de** María*

2. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Wrong possessive agreement: *mi libros* → *mis libros*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Adding <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> to everything: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby><ruby>爸<rt>bà</rt></ruby>*

4. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Possessive for body parts: *mi cabeza duele* → *me duele **la** cabeza*
