## Overview

Serial verb constructions (SVCs) place multiple verbs together in a single clause without explicit conjunctions or infinitive markers.

- **Spanish**: Does not allow true serial verbs. Requires prepositions (*ir a*), conjunctions (*ir y*), or different structures. Multiple verbs must be linked grammatically.
- **English**: Limited serial verbs. Some constructions allow bare infinitives (*go get, come see*), but most require "and" or "to."
- **Chinese**: Extensive serial verb system. Verbs are concatenated freely to express sequence, purpose, direction, result, and giving.

## Spanish

### No true serial verbs

Spanish requires explicit markers between verbs:

**Infinitive with preposition**:

- *Voy **a** comer.* (I'm going to eat.)
- *Empiezo **a** trabajar.* (I start working.)
- *Acabo **de** comer.* (I've just eaten.)
- *Trato **de** entender.* (I try to understand.)

**Conjunctions**:

- *Voy **y** vengo.* (I go and come back.)
- *Entró **y** se sentó.* (He entered and sat down.)

**Different structures**:

- *Dame el libro.* (Give me the book. — one verb, indirect object)
- *Sal **corriendo**.* (He left running. — gerund, not serial verb)

### Perception verb + infinitive

Spanish allows infinitive after perception verbs:

- *Vi **caer** la hoja.* (I saw the leaf fall.)
- *Oí **llorar** al niño.* (I heard the child cry.)

### Causative + infinitive

- *Me hizo **reír**.* (He made me laugh.)
- *Dejó **salir** a los niños.* (He let the children leave.)

## English

### Limited serial verbs

English allows some bare verb sequences in informal registers:

- *Go **get** your coat.*
- *Come **see** me sometime.*
- *Go **ask** your mother.*
- *Run **tell** him.* (Southern US)

**More common**: conjunction or infinitive required

- *Go **and** get your coat.* (British)
- *Come **to** see me.*
- *Try **to** understand.*

### Verb + preposition/gerund

English often uses prepositional phrases instead of serial verbs:

- *Take **out** the trash.* (phrasal verb)
- *Put **on** your shoes.* (phrasal verb)
- *Look **after** the children.* (prepositional verb)

### Causative + bare infinitive

- *He made me **laugh**.*
- *She let him **go**.*
- *I watched him **leave**.*
- *I heard her **sing**.*

### Coordinate structure

- *Go **and** buy some milk.*
- *Come **and** see.*
- *Wait **and** see.*

**Note**: In informal American English, "and" is often dropped: *Go buy some milk.*

## Chinese

### Core serial verb patterns

Chinese concatenates verbs freely:

**Sequence** (do A then B):

- <ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>菜<rt>cài</rt></ruby> (go buy vegetables)
- <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>告<rt>gào</rt></ruby><ruby>诉<rt>su</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby> (run go tell him)
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kan</rt></ruby> (walk over come look-look)

**Purpose** (do A in order to B):

- <ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>医<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>院<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>病<rt>bìng</rt></ruby> (go hospital see illness = go to the hospital to see a doctor)
- <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>中<rt>zhōng</rt></ruby><ruby>文<rt>wén</rt></ruby> (come Beijing study Chinese = come to Beijing to study Chinese)

**Direction** (move + path):

- <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>进<rt>jìn</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (run enter come = run in [toward speaker])
- <ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (walk exit go = walk out [away])
- <ruby>拿<rt>ná</rt></ruby><ruby>回<rt>huí</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (take return come = bring back)

**Giving** (give + recipient + object):

- <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby> (give me one [CL] book)
- <ruby>借<rt>jiè</rt></ruby><ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (lend give him money = lend him money)
- <ruby>送<rt>sòng</rt></ruby><ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>她<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>花<rt>huā</rt></ruby> (give give her flowers = give her flowers)

**Resultative** (verb + result complement):

- <ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>开<rt>kāi</rt></ruby> (hit open = open)
- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> (eat finish = finish eating)
- <ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (do well = do well / finish doing)
- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby> (look perceive = see)
- <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby><ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby> (listen understand = understand by hearing)

### Multiple verb stacking

Chinese can stack many verbs in one clause:

- <ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>医<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>院<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>病<rt>bìng</rt></ruby> (run go hospital see illness)
- <ruby>拿<rt>ná</rt></ruby><ruby>出<rt>chū</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kan</rt></ruby> (take out come give me look-look = take it out and show me)

### Verb + <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>/<ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (purpose)

<ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> and <ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> can indicate purpose:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>学<rt>xué</rt></ruby><ruby>习<rt>xí</rt></ruby> (I come study = I came to study)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>工<rt>gōng</rt></ruby><ruby>作<rt>zuò</rt></ruby> (He go work = He went to work)

### Coverbs

Chinese has "coverbs" that function like prepositions but originated as verbs:

| Coverb | Origin | Function |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> | at (verb) | at, in | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>家<rt>jiā</rt></ruby> (at home) |
| <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby> | give (verb) | to, for | <ruby>给<rt>gěi</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>钱<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (give him money) |
| <ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby> | face (verb) | toward, to | <ruby>对<rt>duì</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby> (speak to him) |
| <ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby> | follow (verb) | with | <ruby>跟<rt>gēn</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby> (go with me) |
| <ruby>比<rt>bǐ</rt></ruby> | compare (verb) | than | <ruby>比<rt>bǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>高<rt>gāo</rt></ruby> (taller than him) |

## Common mistakes

1. **Spanish/English speakers learning Chinese**: Adding conjunctions: *<ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>和<rt>hé</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby>*

2. **Spanish speakers learning Chinese**: Adding <ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby> before purpose verbs: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>医<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>院<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>医<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>院<rt>yuàn</rt></ruby>*

3. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Dropping "and" in formal contexts: *Go buy* is okay in US English but *Go and buy* is preferred formally

4. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using serial verb logic: *Voy comprar* → *Voy **a** comprar*
