## Overview

Aspect expresses how an action relates to time — whether it is completed, ongoing, habitual, or repeated. It is independent of tense (when something happens).

- **Spanish**: Aspect is built into the conjugation system. The preterite vs imperfect distinction is fundamental. Progressive aspect uses *estar + gerund*. Perfect aspect uses *haber + participle*.
- **English**: Aspect is built with auxiliaries. Continuous (*be + -ing*), perfect (*have + -ed*), and combinations (*have been -ing*). Simple aspect is unmarked.
- **Chinese**: Aspect is expressed entirely through **particles** that follow the verb: <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (completion), <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> (ongoing state), <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> (past experience), <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> (ongoing action). The verb never changes.

## Spanish

### Preterite vs imperfect (completed vs ongoing)

The most important aspect distinction in Spanish:

**Preterite** — completed, punctual, with boundaries:

- *Ayer **hablé** con María.* (Yesterday I spoke with María. — one completed conversation)
- *La película **empezó** a las ocho.* (The movie started at eight. — completed event)
- ***Me rompí** la pierna.* (I broke my leg. — completed, resultative)

**Imperfect** — ongoing, habitual, background, no boundaries:

- *Antes **hablaba** con María todos los días.* (I used to speak with María every day. — habitual)
- *La película **empezaba** cuando llegué.* (The movie was starting when I arrived. — ongoing)
- ***Tenía** miedo.* (I was afraid. — state, not event)

**Key uses of imperfect**:

1. **Habitual past**: *Cuando era niño, **iba** a la playa.*
2. **Ongoing background**: ***Llovía** cuando salí.*
3. **Mental/emotional states**: ***Quería** ir.* / ***Era** feliz.*
4. **Physical descriptions**: ***Era** alto y delgado.*
5. **Clock time / age**: ***Eran** las tres.* / ***Tenía** veinte años.*

### Progressive (estar + gerund)

Ongoing action at a specific moment:

- *Estoy **hablando**.* (I am speaking.)
- *Estaba **comiendo**.* (I was eating.)
- *Está **lloviendo**.* (It is raining.)

**Note**: Spanish progressive is more restricted than English. It emphasizes ongoing action, not future plans:

- *Mañana **voy** a Madrid.* (Tomorrow I'm going to Madrid. — NOT estoy yendo)

### Perfect (haber + past participle)

Action with relevance to another time:

- *He **hablado** con ella.* (I have spoken with her. — present relevance)
- *Había **comido** antes.* (I had eaten before. — past relevance)
- *Habrá **terminado** para entonces.* (He will have finished by then. — future relevance)

### Periphrastic aspect constructions

| Construction | Aspect | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ir a + infinitive | prospective | *Voy a comer.* (I'm going to eat.) |
| volver a + infinitive | repetitive | *Vuelvo a intentarlo.* (I try again.) |
| acabar de + infinitive | recent past | *Acabo de llegar.* (I just arrived.) |
| estar por + infinitive | imminent | *Estoy por salir.* (I'm about to leave.) |
| seguir + gerund | continuative | *Sigue lloviendo.* (It keeps raining.) |
| dejar de + infinitive | cessation | *Dejó de fumar.* (He stopped smoking.) |
| empezar a + infinitive | ingressive | *Empecé a estudiar.* (I started studying.) |

## English

### Simple aspect (unmarked)

The default — no special marking:

- *I speak Spanish.* (habitual)
- *I spoke to her.* (completed past)
- *I will call tomorrow.* (future)

### Continuous (be + -ing)

Action in progress at a specific time:

- *I **am speaking**.* (present continuous)
- *I **was speaking**.* (past continuous)
- *I **will be speaking**.* (future continuous)

**Uses**:

1. **Action in progress**: *She **is sleeping** right now.*
2. **Temporary situation**: *I **am living** in Madrid.*
3. **Future arrangement**: *I **am meeting** her at 3.*
4. **Changing situation**: *The climate **is getting** warmer.*
5. **Habitual with always** (complaint): *He **is always leaving** his stuff everywhere.*

**Stative verbs** rarely take continuous:

- ✅ *I **know** him.* (NOT *I am knowing him.*)
- ✅ *I **love** chocolate.* (NOT *I am loving chocolate.* — except McDonald's slogan)
- ✅ *I **understand**.* (NOT *I am understanding.*)

### Perfect (have + -ed)

Action completed before another time, with relevance:

- *I **have spoken** to her.* (present perfect — past action, present relevance)
- *I **had spoken** to her.* (past perfect — before another past action)
- *I **will have spoken** to her.* (future perfect — before another future action)

**Present perfect uses**:

1. **Life experience**: *I **have been** to China.*
2. **Recent past with now**: *I **have just finished**.*
3. **Unfinished time**: *I **have seen** three movies this week.* (week not over)
4. **Result now**: *I **have lost** my keys.* (still lost)

**Past simple vs present perfect**:

- *I **saw** that movie.* (when? unspecified, finished)
- *I **have seen** that movie.* (in my life, experience)

### Perfect continuous (have been + -ing)

Ongoing activity up to now:

- *I **have been waiting** for an hour.* (and still waiting)
- *She **had been working** there for years.* (before she quit)

### Used to / would (habitual past)

| Form | Usage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| used to + verb | past habit, no longer true | *I **used to** smoke.* |
| would + verb | past habit, with time reference | *Every summer we **would** go camping.* |
| past simple | past habit (also valid) | *I smoked for ten years.* |

**Note**: *Would* for habits requires a time reference. *Used to* does not.

## Chinese

### <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> — completion / change

<ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> is the most complex aspect particle:

**Verb + <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>** = completed action:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby>。(I ate [and finished].)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(He bought a book.)

**Sentence-final <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>** = change of state:

- <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(It's raining now. — change from not raining)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(I understand now. — change from not understanding)

**Both <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>** = completed action + change:

- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(I've eaten already.)

### <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> — past experience

<ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> indicates the action has happened at some time (experience):

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>。(I've been to Beijing. — experience)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>中<rt>zhōng</rt></ruby><ruby>餐<rt>cān</rt></ruby>。(He's eaten Chinese food.)

**Compare <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> and <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby>**:

- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> (ate — completed, specific)
- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> (have eaten — experience, non-specific)

### <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> — ongoing action

<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> marks progressive aspect:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby>。(I'm eating.)
- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>工<rt>gōng</rt></ruby><ruby>作<rt>zuò</rt></ruby>。(He's working.)

**<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> + <ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>** for emphasis:

- <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>雨<rt>yǔ</rt></ruby><ruby>呢<rt>ne</rt></ruby>！(It's raining! — ongoing, emphatic)

### <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> — continuous state

<ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> indicates a continuing state or accompanying action:

- <ruby>门<rt>mén</rt></ruby><ruby>开<rt>kāi</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby>。(The door is open. — state)
- <ruby>她<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby><ruby>红<rt>hóng</rt></ruby><ruby>衣<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby>。(She's wearing red clothes. — state)
- <ruby>笑<rt>xiào</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby><ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby> (say with a smile — accompanying action)

**<ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> vs <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>**:

- <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby> (in the process of putting on)
- <ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> (wearing — state)

### <ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> / <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> — directional aspect

<ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (beginning): <ruby>唱<rt>chàng</rt></ruby><ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> (start singing)

<ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (continuing): <ruby>说<rt>shuō</rt></ruby><ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> (continue speaking)

### <ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> / <ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> / <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby> — resultative complements

Chinese expresses result through verb + complement:

| Complement | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| <ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> | finished | <ruby>做<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby> (finish doing) |
| <ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> | completed well | <ruby>写<rt>xiě</rt></ruby><ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby> (finish writing) |
| <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby> | achieved/reached | <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby> (succeed in seeing) |
| <ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby> | perceived | <ruby>听<rt>tīng</rt></ruby><ruby>见<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby> (hear) |
| <ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby> | understood | <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>懂<rt>dǒng</rt></ruby> (understand by reading) |

### Habitual aspect (no marker)

Chinese expresses habit through adverbs, not verb morphology:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>经<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>常<rt>cháng</rt></ruby><ruby>喝<rt>hē</rt></ruby><ruby>咖<rt>kā</rt></ruby><ruby>啡<rt>fēi</rt></ruby>。(I often drink coffee.)
- <ruby>每<rt>měi</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>跑<rt>pǎo</rt></ruby><ruby>步<rt>bù</rt></ruby>。(I run every day.)

## Comparison at a glance

| Aspect | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Completed | Preterite | Past simple / present perfect | <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby> |
| Ongoing | Estar + gerund | Be + -ing | <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> / <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> |
| Habitual | Imperfect | Used to / would / past simple | Adverbs (<ruby>经<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>常<rt>cháng</rt></ruby>, <ruby>每<rt>měi</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby>) |
| Past experience | Perfect (he hablado) | Present perfect (have spoken) | <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> |
| Resultative | Estar + participle / perfect | Have + -ed / be + -ed | Complements (<ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby>, <ruby>好<rt>hǎo</rt></ruby>, <ruby>到<rt>dào</rt></ruby>) |
| Beginning | Empezar a | Start to / start -ing | <ruby>起<rt>qǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby> |
| Continuative | Seguir + gerund | Keep -ing / continue to | <ruby>下<rt>xià</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> |
| Repetitive | Volver a | Again / re- | <ruby>再<rt>zài</rt></ruby> / <ruby>又<rt>yòu</rt></ruby> |
| Verb change | Yes (conjugation) | Yes (auxiliaries) | No (particles only) |

### I ate (completed)
- **ES**: *Comí.* (preterite)
- **EN**: *I ate.* / *I have eaten.*
- **ZH**: <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。

- **ES**: *Estaba comiendo.*

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: Using preterite for habitual: *Cuando era niño, fui a la playa* → *...**iba** a la playa*

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: Using continuous with stative verbs: *I am knowing him* → *I **know** him*

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: Overusing <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>: *<ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby><ruby>商<rt>shāng</rt></ruby><ruby>店<rt>diàn</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>商<rt>shāng</rt></ruby><ruby>店<rt>diàn</rt></ruby>* (simple narrative)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: Omitting continuous: *I eat now* → *I **am eating** now*
