## Overview

Non-finite verbs are verb forms that do not show tense, person, or number.

- **Spanish**: Uses the **infinitive** (-ar/-er/-ir) for most non-finite functions. The **gerund** (-ando/-iendo) is used for progressive aspect and some adjuncts. No separate form like English -ing.
- **English**: Uses **-ing** forms extensively — as gerunds (nouns), present participles (progressive), and adjectives. Also uses the **infinitive** (to + verb).
- **Chinese**: Has no infinitive or gerund morphology. Verbs are used directly. Aspect markers (<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby>, <ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>, <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby>) and result complements add temporal meaning.

## Spanish

### Infinitive

The infinitive is the default non-finite form in Spanish:

**As subject**:

- *Nadar es saludable.* (Swimming is healthy.)
- *Fumar es malo.* (Smoking is bad.)
- *Estudiar requiere paciencia.* (Studying requires patience.)

**As object of verb**:

- *Quiero **ir**.* (I want to go.)
- *Debes **estudiar**.* (You must study.)
- *Prefiero **cantar**.* (I prefer to sing.)
- *Me gusta **bailar**.* (I like dancing.)

**After preposition**:

- *antes de **comer*** (before eating)
- *después de **llegar*** (after arriving)
- *sin **decir** nada* (without saying anything)
- *para **ayudarte*** (to help you)
- *al **entrar*** (upon entering)

### Gerund (-ando / -iendo)

The Spanish gerund is used for:

**Progressive aspect** (with estar):

- *Estoy **comiendo**.* (I am eating.)
- *Estaba **leyendo**.* (I was reading.)
- *Están **durmiendo**.* (They are sleeping.)

**Simultaneous action**:

- *Se fue **cantando**.* (He left singing.)
- *Llegó **corriendo**.* (He arrived running.)

**Manner**:

- *Habla **gritando**.* (He speaks shouting.)

**Cause**:

- ***Estando** enfermo, no pudo venir.* (Being sick, he couldn't come.)

### Not a noun

Unlike English, the Spanish gerund is **never** a noun:

- ❌ *El fumando es malo.* (WRONG)
- ✅ *El **fumar** es malo.* (The infinitive is used.)

### Irregular gerunds

| Verb | Gerund |
|---|---|
| dormir | durmiendo |
| morir | muriendo |
| pedir | pidiendo |
| servir | sirviendo |
| vestir | vistiendo |
| sentir | sintiendo |
| reír | riendo |
| sonreír | sonriendo |
| ir | yendo |
| venir | viniendo |
| decir | diciendo |
| poder | pudiendo |

### Infinitive vs gerund with perception verbs

| Structure | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Lo vi **hacer** algo. | I saw him do something. (completed) |
| Lo vi **haciendo** algo. | I saw him doing something. (in progress) |

## English

### Gerund (-ing as noun)

The gerund functions as a noun:

**As subject**:

- ***Swimming** is healthy.*
- ***Reading** is fun.*
- ***Learning** languages takes time.*

**As object**:

- *I enjoy **swimming**.*
- *She hates **waiting**.*
- *He admitted **stealing** the money.*

**After preposition**:

- *interested in **learning***
- *good at **swimming***
- *tired of **waiting***
- *afraid of **flying***
- *instead of **eating***
- *without **saying** anything*

### Present participle (-ing as adjective/progressive)

**Progressive aspect**:

- *I am **eating**.*
- *She was **reading**.*
- *They are **sleeping**.*

**Adjectival**:

- *a **swimming** pool*
- *a **sleeping** child*
- *a **running** joke*
- ***boring** vs **bored***

### Verbal noun vs common noun

| Gerund (verbal) | Common noun |
|---|---|
| *No **smoking** is allowed.* | *There is no **smoke**.* |
| *I enjoy **swimming**.* | *I went for a **swim**.* |
| ***Running** is hard.* | *I went for a **run**.* |

### Infinitive (to + verb)

**After certain verbs**:

- *I want **to go**.*
- *She decided **to leave**.*
- *He promised **to help**.*

**After adjectives**:

- *It's important **to study**.*
- *I'm happy **to see** you.*

**Purpose**:

- *I came **to help**.*
- *This is **to inform** you.*

### Gerund vs infinitive after verbs

Some verbs change meaning:

| Verb + gerund | Verb + infinitive |
|---|---|
| I stopped **smoking**. (I quit.) | I stopped **to smoke**. (I paused in order to smoke.) |
| I remembered **locking** the door. (memory of action) | I remembered **to lock** the door. (I didn't forget.) |
| I tried **opening** it. (experiment) | I tried **to open** it. (attempt) |
| I regret **telling** him. (past action) | I regret **to inform** you. (formal bad news) |

### -ing after prepositions

English always uses -ing after prepositions:

- *before **leaving***
- *after **arriving***
- *without **asking***
- *instead of **working***

## Chinese

### No non-finite morphology

Chinese verbs have no special non-finite form:

- <ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby><ruby>很<rt>hěn</rt></ruby><ruby>健<rt>jiàn</rt></ruby><ruby>康<rt>kāng</rt></ruby>。(Swimming is healthy. — verb used directly)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huan</rt></ruby><ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby>。(I like swimming. — verb used directly)

### <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> for progressive

<ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> marks progressive aspect:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby>。(He is eating.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>。(I am reading.)

<ruby>正<rt>zhèng</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> emphasizes in-progress:

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>正<rt>zhèng</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>开<rt>kāi</rt></ruby><ruby>会<rt>huì</rt></ruby>。(He is in a meeting right now.)

### Verb + <ruby>着<rt>zháo</rt></ruby> for durative state

<ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> marks a continuous state:

- <ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby><ruby>衣<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby> (wearing clothes — state)
- <ruby>坐<rt>zuò</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby> (sitting — state)
- <ruby>开<rt>kāi</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby><ruby>门<rt>mén</rt></ruby> (with the door open — state)

**Compare**:

- <ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby><ruby>衣<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby> (put on clothes — action)
- <ruby>穿<rt>chuān</rt></ruby><ruby>着<rt>zhe</rt></ruby><ruby>衣<rt>yī</rt></ruby><ruby>服<rt>fu</rt></ruby> (wearing clothes — state)

### Verb + <ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> for experience

<ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby> marks experienced action:

- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>北<rt>běi</rt></ruby><ruby>京<rt>jīng</rt></ruby>。(I've been to Beijing.)
- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby> (have eaten)

### <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>/<ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby> as purpose markers

- <ruby>去<rt>qù</rt></ruby><ruby>买<rt>mǎi</rt></ruby><ruby>东<rt>dōng</rt></ruby><ruby>西<rt>xi</rt></ruby> (go to buy things)
- <ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>你<rt>nǐ</rt></ruby> (come to see you)

### <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hòu</rt></ruby> for temporal clauses

- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hou</rt></ruby> (when eating)
- <ruby>看<rt>kàn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>时<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>候<rt>hou</rt></ruby> (while reading)

### Coverbs instead of prepositions + gerunds

Chinese uses coverbs rather than preposition + gerund:

- <ruby>吃<rt>chī</rt></ruby><ruby>完<rt>wán</rt></ruby><ruby>饭<rt>fàn</rt></ruby><ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>后<rt>hòu</rt></ruby> (after eating — <ruby>以<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>后<rt>hòu</rt></ruby> = after)
- <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>觉<rt>jiào</rt></ruby><ruby>之<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby> (before sleeping — <ruby>之<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>前<rt>qián</rt></ruby> = before)

| Function | Spanish | English | Chinese |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| As subject | Infinitive | Gerund (-ing) | Verb directly |

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Nadando es bueno* → *Nadar es bueno* (infinitive as subject)

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *I enjoy to swim* → *I enjoy **swimming*** (gerund after enjoy)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huān</rt></ruby><ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby><ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby>* → *<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>喜<rt>xǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>欢<rt>huān</rt></ruby><ruby>游<rt>yóu</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>yǒng</rt></ruby>* (no <ruby>在<rt>zài</rt></ruby> needed for general liking)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *I like swim* → *I like **swimming*** / *I like **to swim***
