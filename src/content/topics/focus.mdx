## Overview

Focus marks the most important or new information in an utterance.

- **Spanish**: Uses word order flexibility, stress, clefts (*fue...quien*), and particles (*sí, solo, ni siquiera*). Pro-drop allows subject omission, making focused subjects more prominent.
- **English**: Uses stress (pitch accent), clefts (*it was...who/that*), pseudo-clefts (*what...was*), do-support for verum focus, and particles (*only, even, just*).
- **Chinese**: Uses the *<ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>* cleft construction, adverbs (*<ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby>, <ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby>, <ruby>只<rt>zhī</rt></ruby>*), and the *<ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby>...<ruby>都<rt>dū</rt></ruby>/<ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby>* construction. Stress is less available due to the tonal system.

## Spanish

### Stress focus

Any element can receive stress:

- ***JUAN** lo rompió.* (JOHN broke it. — focus on subject)
- *Juan **LO** rompió.* (John broke IT. — focus on object)
- *Juan lo **ROMPIÓ**.* (John BROKE it. — focus on verb)
- ***AYER** Juan lo rompió.* (YESTERDAY John broke it. — focus on time)

### Cleft sentences

**Subject cleft**:
- *Fue **JUAN** quien lo rompió.* (It was JOHN who broke it.)
- *Fue **MARÍA** la que ganó.* (It was MARÍA who won.)

**Object cleft**:
- *Fue **EL JARRÓN** lo que rompió Juan.* (It was THE VASE that John broke.)
- *Fue **EN MADRID** donde nos conocimos.* (It was IN MADRID where we met.)

**Time cleft**:
- *Fue **AYER** cuando llegó.* (It was YESTERDAY when he arrived.)

### Pseudo-clefts

- ***Lo que** me molesta es el ruido.* (What bothers me is the noise.)
- ***Lo que** quiero es paz.* (What I want is peace.)
- ***El que** vino fue Juan.* (The one who came was Juan.)

### Verum focus (sí)

- *Juan **sí** lo rompió.* (John DID break it.)
- *Yo **sí** quiero ir.* (I DO want to go.)
- ***Sí** que es verdad.* (It IS true.)

### Exclusive focus (solo / únicamente)

- ***Solo** Juan lo sabía.* (Only John knew.)
- ***Únicamente** María vino.* (Only María came.)
- *Vino **nada más** que Juan.* (Only Juan came.)

### Additive focus (incluso / hasta)

- ***Incluso** Juan no pudo hacerlo.* (Even John couldn't do it.)
- ***Hasta** María lo intentó.* (Even María tried it.)
- *Ni **siquiera** Juan...* (Not even John...)

### Negative focus (ni...ni / tampoco)

- ***Ni** Juan **ni** María vinieron.* (Neither John nor María came.)
- *María **tampoco** vino.* (María didn't come either.)

### Fronting without cleft

- *A **JUAN** le di el libro.* (To JOHN I gave the book.)
- ***EL LIBRO** le di a Juan.* (THE BOOK I gave to Juan.)

## English

### Stress focus (pitch accent)

- ***JOHN** broke it.* (not someone else)
- *John broke **IT**.* (not something else)
- *John **BROKE** it.* (he didn't steal it)
- *John broke it **YESTERDAY**.* (not today)

### It-cleft

- *It was **JOHN** who broke it.*
- *It was **THE VASE** that John broke.*
- *It was **YESTERDAY** that he arrived.*
- *It was **IN MADRID** where we met.*

### Pseudo-cleft

- ***What** I want is peace.*
- ***What** bothers me is the noise.*
- ***The one** who came was John.*
- ***All** I need is love.*

### Reversed pseudo-cleft

- *Peace is ***what** I want.*
- *John is ***the one** who came.*

### Do-support for verum focus

- *John **DID** break it.*
- *I **DO** want to go.*
- *It **IS** true.*
- *She **DOES** speak French.*

**Note**: Do-support is the only way to mark verum focus unambiguously in English.

### Only (exclusive focus)

- ***Only** John knew.*
- *John **only** broke the vase.* (he didn't do anything else)
- *John broke **only** the vase.* (he didn't break anything else)

### Even (additive focus)

- ***Even** John couldn't do it.*
- *John couldn't do it, **even** with help.*
- ***Not even** John knew.*

### Also / too (additive)

- *John **also** broke it.*
- *John broke it **too**.*

### Inversion for focus

- ***Never** have I seen such beauty.*
- ***Rarely** does he complain.*
- ***Only then** did I understand.*

## Chinese

### <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby>...<ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> cleft (most common)

- <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was JOHN who broke it.)
- <ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>花<rt>huā</rt></ruby><ruby>瓶<rt>píng</rt></ruby>。(What John broke was THE VASE.)
- <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It was YESTERDAY that he came.)

**Note**: <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby> marks the focused element; <ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> nominalizes the presupposition.

### <ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby> (immediate focus)

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>个<rt>ge</rt></ruby>。(He wants exactly THIS.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>就<rt>jiù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby><ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>会<rt>huì</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>lái</rt></ruby>。(I KNEW he would come.)

### <ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby> (delayed/conditional focus)

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>昨<rt>zuó</rt></ruby><ruby>天<rt>tiān</rt></ruby><ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>zǒu</rt></ruby>。(He ONLY left yesterday.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>十<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>点<rt>diǎn</rt></ruby><ruby>才<rt>cái</rt></ruby><ruby>睡<rt>shuì</rt></ruby><ruby>觉<rt>jiào</rt></ruby>。(I didn't sleep until 10 o'clock.)

### <ruby>只<rt>zhī</rt></ruby> / <ruby>只<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby> (exclusive)

- <ruby>只<rt>zhǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby><ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(Only John knew.)
- <ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>只<rt>zhǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>想<rt>xiǎng</rt></ruby><ruby>要<rt>yào</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yí</rt></ruby><ruby>杯<rt>bēi</rt></ruby><ruby>水<rt>shuǐ</rt></ruby>。(I only want a glass of water.)

### <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby>...<ruby>都<rt>dū</rt></ruby>/<ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby> (additive focus)

- <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby><ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>都<rt>dōu</rt></ruby><ruby>不<rt>bù</rt></ruby><ruby>知<rt>zhī</rt></ruby><ruby>道<rt>dào</rt></ruby>。(Even John doesn't know.)
- <ruby>连<rt>lián</rt></ruby><ruby>一<rt>yì</rt></ruby><ruby>杯<rt>bēi</rt></ruby><ruby>水<rt>shuǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>也<rt>yě</rt></ruby><ruby>没<rt>méi</rt></ruby><ruby>有<rt>yǒu</rt></ruby>。(Not even a glass of water.)

### <ruby>确<rt>què</rt></ruby><ruby>实<rt>shí</rt></ruby> / <ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (verum focus)

- <ruby>他<rt>tā</rt></ruby><ruby>确<rt>què</rt></ruby><ruby>实<rt>shí</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(He DID break it.)
- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>真<rt>zhēn</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby>。(It IS true.)

### Topic fronting (focus on comment)

- <ruby>这<rt>zhè</rt></ruby><ruby>本<rt>běn</rt></ruby><ruby>书<rt>shū</rt></ruby>，<ruby>我<rt>wǒ</rt></ruby><ruby>已<rt>yǐ</rt></ruby><ruby>经<rt>jīng</rt></ruby><ruby>读<rt>dú</rt></ruby><ruby>过<rt>guò</rt></ruby><ruby>了<rt>le</rt></ruby>。(This book, I have already read. — book = topic, read = focus)

## Common mistakes

1. **English speakers learning Spanish**: *Es Juan que lo rompió* → *Fue **Juan quien** lo rompió*

2. **Spanish speakers learning English**: *Juan sí lo rompió* → *John **DID** break it* (do-support)

3. **English speakers learning Chinese**: *<ruby>约<rt>yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>它<rt>tā</rt></ruby> (stress)* → <ruby>是<rt>shì</rt></ruby><ruby>约<rt>Yuē</rt></ruby><ruby>翰<rt>hàn</rt></ruby><ruby>打<rt>dǎ</rt></ruby><ruby>破<rt>pò</rt></ruby><ruby>的<rt>de</rt></ruby> (cleft construction)

4. **Chinese speakers learning English**: *Only John know* → *Only John **knew*** (agreement)
